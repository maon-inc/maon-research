## Summary

The first systematic review and meta-analysis examining the impact of ADHD on cardiac vagal control during task demand. Finds reduced vagally-mediated HRV in ADHD during cognitive tasks, with a small but significant effect size.

## Key Findings

- ADHD patients show reduced vagally-mediated HRV during tasks (Hedge's g = 0.20, CI 0.01-0.40)
- 13 studies, 869 ADHD patients, 909 healthy controls
- HRV differences are more apparent during task performance than at rest
- Resting-state HRV may not differ significantly in ADHD (task-specific effect)
- Task type, respiration rate, and comorbid disorders are significant moderators

## MAON Applicability

Important for MAON's interpretation of HRV in ADHD users. MAON should not expect dramatically different resting HRV in ADHD users. However, if MAON can identify periods of cognitive demand (e.g., via calendar meetings or screen time patterns), HRV during those periods may be more informative.

## Methodology Notes

Meta-analysis published in Neuroscience and Biobehavioral Reviews (2019). 13 studies. Distinguishes resting vs. task-related HRV. High heterogeneity noted (I² = 77%).
