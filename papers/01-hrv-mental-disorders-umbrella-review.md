## Summary

This umbrella review synthesizes multiple meta-analyses examining HRV across various mental disorders. It found that reduced HRV is consistently associated with psychiatric conditions including depression, anxiety, and PTSD, though evidence strength varies by disorder. This is the most comprehensive overview of HRV-mental health associations available.

## Key Findings

- HRV reductions are observed across multiple psychiatric disorders, with the strongest evidence for PTSD and anxiety disorders
- Evidence for HRV differences between major depressive disorder patients and healthy controls was present but below the suggestive level of significance
- Autonomic dysregulation indexed by HRV is a transdiagnostic marker across mental health conditions
- Different HRV metrics (time-domain vs frequency-domain) show varying sensitivity across disorders

## MAON Applicability

MAON can use this as a foundational reference for interpreting HRV changes across all self-reported conditions. The transdiagnostic nature of HRV reductions means MAON should flag sustained HRV decreases regardless of the user's specific condition, while calibrating sensitivity based on the disorder-specific effect sizes reported.

## Methodology Notes

Umbrella review of meta-analyses published in Translational Psychiatry (2025). Synthesizes findings across multiple psychiatric conditions with graded evidence levels. High methodological rigor as it reviews only prior meta-analyses.
