## Summary

A systematic review examining disruptions in the rest-activity cycle in bipolar disorder as measured by actigraphy. Provides a framework for digital assessment of circadian disruption in bipolar patients using wearable devices.

## Key Findings

- Bipolar disorder is characterized by disrupted rest-activity patterns detectable by actigraphy
- Increased sleep fragmentation index distinguishes bipolar from healthy controls
- Higher nocturnal activity (least active 5-hour period) is a marker of bipolar disorder
- Lower circadian relative amplitude compared to controls
- Circadian disruption may precede mood episodes, offering predictive value

## MAON Applicability

For users who self-report bipolar disorder, MAON should monitor rest-activity rhythm regularity via Apple Watch accelerometry. Changes in nocturnal activity or circadian amplitude could serve as early warning signals for mood episodes.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review published in Frontiers in Psychiatry (2022). Covers multiple actigraphy studies in bipolar disorder. Open access. Provides digital assessment framework.
