## Summary

Examines the dynamical association between physical activity and affect (mood) in daily life for individuals with ADHD using ecological momentary assessment combined with accelerometry.

## Key Findings

- Physical activity has immediate positive effects on affect (mood) in individuals with ADHD
- The activity-mood relationship is detectable in real-time daily life settings
- Higher activity levels predict better mood in subsequent assessments
- The effect is bidirectional but physical activity is a stronger predictor of mood than vice versa
- Accelerometer-measured activity provides objective real-time data

## MAON Applicability

Provides evidence for MAON's real-time intervention approach for ADHD users. When MAON detects declining mood signals alongside low activity, it can suggest physical activity as an immediate mood-lifting strategy, backed by this daily-life evidence.

## Methodology Notes

Published in European Neuropsychopharmacology (2022). EMA + accelerometry design. Daily life measurement. ADHD-specific population. Ecological validity.
