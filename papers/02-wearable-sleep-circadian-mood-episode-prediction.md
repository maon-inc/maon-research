## Summary

This landmark study demonstrates that wearable-collected sleep and circadian rhythm features can accurately predict next-day mood episodes (depressive, manic, hypomanic) in patients with bipolar disorder and major depression. Used longitudinal data from 168 patients.

## Key Findings

- Next-day prediction AUCs: depressive episodes 0.80, manic episodes 0.98, hypomanic episodes 0.95
- Daily circadian phase shifts were the most significant predictors: delays linked to depressive episodes, advances to manic episodes
- 36 sleep and circadian rhythm features were derived from wearable data
- Models predict future episodes using only sleep-wake data easily gathered through smartphones and wearables
- Average 587 days clinical follow-up, 267 days wearable data per patient

## MAON Applicability

Directly validates MAON's ability to predict mood episodes from sleep data. MAON should monitor circadian phase shifts (sleep timing changes) as a primary predictor, and can achieve clinical-grade prediction accuracy for manic/hypomanic episodes using wearable data alone.

## Methodology Notes

Prospective observational cohort study with 168 mood disorder patients. Published in npj Digital Medicine (2024). Mathematical modeling approach. Longitudinal wearable data collection. High clinical relevance.
