## Summary

A systematic review and meta-analysis of 23 studies (n=2,563) evaluating just-in-time adaptive interventions (JITAIs) and ecological momentary interventions (EMIs) for mental health outcomes. Published in BMJ Mental Health, this is the first meta-analytic synthesis of JITAI effectiveness across mental health conditions, finding a small but significant overall effect with stronger results for mental illness reduction than general well-being improvement.

## Key Findings

- 23 studies with 2,563 participants (71.7% female) were included, published between 2018 and May 2025
- JITAIs/EMIs showed a small but significant between-group effect overall (g=0.15, 95% CI 0.05 to 0.26, p=0.003)
- Significant effect for improving mental illness symptoms (g=0.21, 95% CI 0.10 to 0.31, p<0.001)
- No significant effect on general psychological well-being (g=0.03)
- Interventions shorter than 6 weeks yielded greater longevity of effects (g=0.71, 95% CI 0.18 to 1.24, p=0.008)
- Over half of included studies (52.2%) were published in or after 2024, reflecting rapid growth in the field

## MAON Applicability

Directly validates MAON's intervention system, which delivers calm (slow-breathing) and attention interventions triggered by physiological signals. The finding that shorter intervention periods show stronger lasting effects supports MAON's approach of brief, targeted micro-interventions (30-45 second durations). The significant effect on mental illness symptoms (rather than general well-being) suggests MAON should prioritize intervention delivery during detected stress or mood disturbance episodes rather than routine wellness prompts.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review following PRISMA guidelines with meta-analysis. Searched multiple databases with structured queries covering JITAI and EMI terminology. Risk of bias assessed using Cochrane tools. Heterogeneity was moderate across studies. Subgroup analyses examined intervention duration, delivery modality, and target condition. Effect sizes calculated as Hedges' g for between-group comparisons.
