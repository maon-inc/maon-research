## Summary

Study demonstrating that teaching professionals with burnout exhibit higher average heart rates, fewer daily steps, and more executive function challenges compared to non-burned-out peers, with all measures captured via wearable devices in daily life.

## Key Findings

- Subjects with burnout had significantly higher average heart rates in daily life than those without burnout
- Burned-out individuals took fewer daily steps, indicating reduced physical activity
- Executive functioning was impaired in burnout, suggesting cognitive effects detectable alongside physiological ones
- These alterations are easily detectable with consumer wearable devices
- Combination of elevated HR + reduced steps may serve as a novel burnout biomarker

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks both HR and daily steps. The finding that elevated HR combined with decreased step count signals burnout gives MAON a concrete multi-signal pattern to detect. This is a high-value compound indicator.

## Methodology Notes

Cross-sectional study of teaching professionals. Published in IJERPH (2022). Used wearable HR monitoring and step counting alongside validated burnout questionnaires. Initial evidence requiring replication.
