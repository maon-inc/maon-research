## Summary

A comprehensive review in Translational Psychiatry examining the bidirectional relationship between circadian rhythm disruption and mental health. Covers night shift work, jet lag, artificial light exposure, and their effects on mood disorders.

## Key Findings

- Bidirectional relationship between mood disorders and circadian rhythms
- Disrupted circadian clock-controlled responses (sleep, cortisol) are associated with mood disorders
- Jet lag, night-shift work, and artificial light at night can precipitate or exacerbate affective symptoms
- Disruption of circadian rhythms via shift work increases risk of depression and anxiety
- Low-cost behavioral and lifestyle changes can improve circadian rhythms and behavioral health

## MAON Applicability

MAON can detect circadian disruption patterns (irregular sleep timing, late-night screen use, disrupted rest-activity rhythms) that this paper links to mental health deterioration. Provides evidence base for MAON's circadian-focused nudges.
