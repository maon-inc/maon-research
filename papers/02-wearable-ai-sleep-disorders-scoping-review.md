## Summary

A scoping review examining the application of AI and machine learning to wearable-derived sleep data for detecting and monitoring sleep disorders. Covers the landscape of algorithms, signal types, and clinical applications.

## Key Findings

- AI models applied to wearable sleep data can detect multiple sleep disorders with clinically useful accuracy
- Combining accelerometer and PPG signals improves sleep staging and disorder detection over accelerometer-only approaches
- Deep learning approaches show promise for personalized sleep analysis
- Consumer wearables are increasingly capable of supporting clinical-grade sleep assessment when paired with appropriate algorithms

## MAON Applicability

Validates MAON's ML-driven approach to sleep quality scoring. The review's findings on multi-signal AI models support MAON's strategy of combining sleep stage data with HR/HRV/respiratory rate for a composite sleep quality score.

## Methodology Notes

Scoping review published in JMIR (2025). Covers wearable AI for sleep disorders broadly. Open access. Focuses on algorithmic approaches rather than specific device validation.
