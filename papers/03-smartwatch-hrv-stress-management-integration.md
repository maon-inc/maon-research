## Summary

A review examining the integration of smartwatches and HRV technology for real-time stress management. Covers how consumer smartwatches measure stress through HRV and the potential for just-in-time interventions.

## Key Findings

- Smartwatches can provide real-time stress detection through continuous HRV monitoring
- Low HRV during sleep is associated with higher mental stress levels
- LF/HF ratio reflects sympathetic-parasympathetic balance and serves as a stress indicator
- Longitudinal monitoring of resting HR and HRV identifies trends, tracks recovery, and enables personalized feedback
- AI-trained models combining HRV with other physiological signals can identify emotional stress patterns in real time

## MAON Applicability

Validates MAON's core architecture of using smartwatch HRV for stress detection and just-in-time interventions via messaging. The personalized feedback approach aligns with MAON's z-score baseline methodology.

## Methodology Notes

Review article published in Sensors (2023). Covers multiple smartwatch platforms including Apple Watch, Garmin, Fitbit, Polar, and Samsung. Focuses on practical integration rather than clinical validation.
