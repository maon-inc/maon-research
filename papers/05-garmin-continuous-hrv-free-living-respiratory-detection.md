## Summary

This study demonstrated a protocol for continuous heart rate variability monitoring using Garmin Fenix 6 smartwatches in free-living conditions over an 8-month period. The research focused on prospective health anomaly detection, particularly for viral respiratory infections such as influenza and COVID-19, using an automated data collection, metrics extraction, and analysis pipeline.

## Key Findings

- 59 participants provided daily smartwatch data and health symptom/illness reports over 8 months of continuous monitoring
- Garmin Fenix 6 smartwatches via PPG sensors successfully captured high-resolution heart inter-beat interval (IBI) and step count data in free-living conditions
- The study was the first to demonstrate feasibility of measuring high-resolution IBI using consumer smartwatches in near real-time for respiratory illness detection over long-term monitoring
- Automated pipeline enabled prospective analysis compatible with near real-time health assessment
- HRV metrics extracted from consumer-grade wearables showed meaningful changes during illness onset
- Long-term free-living monitoring was feasible with acceptable data quality despite the challenges of real-world wear conditions

## MAON Applicability

Directly relevant to MAON's integration of Garmin wearable data and its use of continuous HRV monitoring. The validated data collection pipeline and HRV metric extraction approach can inform MAON's own Garmin data processing. The health anomaly detection methodology aligns with MAON's goal of detecting changes in user wellbeing through physiological signals. The 8-month longitudinal design demonstrates the feasibility of the kind of sustained monitoring MAON aims to provide.

## Methodology Notes

Exploratory observational study with 59 participants monitored for 8 months. Data collected from Garmin Fenix 6 smartwatches using PPG sensors. Participants provided daily health symptom and illness reports. Automated pipeline for data collection, HRV metrics extraction, and prospective health anomaly analysis. Published in JMIR Formative Research, August 2024.
