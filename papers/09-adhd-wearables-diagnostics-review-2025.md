## Summary

A comprehensive review of wearable technology applications for ADHD spanning 2013 to early 2025. Covers accelerometry, cardiac, electrodermal, and EEG-based wearables for ADHD assessment, continuous monitoring, and real-time intervention delivery. The review highlights that wearable-derived metrics (motor activity, autonomic arousal, attentional lapses) align with symptom trajectories and medication effects, while early intervention trials explore haptic prompts and attention-supporting apps delivered through head-worn devices.

## Key Findings

- Accelerometers, heart rate/HRV sensors, and electrodermal activity sensors can capture ecologically valid ADHD markers in everyday settings
- Continuous actigraphy monitoring reveals greater motor activity, longer sleep onset latency, and lower sleep efficiency in ADHD vs. controls
- Wearable-derived autonomic metrics (HRV) correlate with ADHD symptom severity and medication response in ambulatory settings
- Emotional dysregulation in ADHD manifests in HRV and electrodermal activity patterns, making wearables suitable for tracking it
- Early intervention trials show promise for haptic wrist-based prompts and attention cues delivered via consumer wearables
- Real-world ecological validity is a major advantage of wearables over clinic-based assessment for ADHD

## MAON Applicability

MAON's Apple Watch integration and continuous HRV and activity monitoring map directly onto the sensor modalities this review validates for ADHD. The finding that autonomic metrics align with medication effects and symptom trajectories supports MAON's value as a daily ADHD companion tool. The review also validates MAON's proactive messaging approach as an analog to haptic intervention delivery.
