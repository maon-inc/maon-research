## Summary

A systematic review and meta-analysis examining the combined effects of sleep duration, chronotype, and social jetlag on depression risk. Integrates multiple circadian-related risk factors.

## Key Findings

- Evening chronotype is associated with increased depression risk
- Both short and long sleep duration increase depression risk (U-shaped relationship)
- Social jetlag independently contributes to depression risk
- The combination of evening chronotype + social jetlag + irregular sleep creates compounding risk
- Circadian factors interact with sleep duration in predicting depression

## MAON Applicability

MAON can infer chronotype from sleep timing patterns and detect social jetlag from weekday/weekend differences. This paper supports MAON using circadian profile as a risk modifier in its pattern interpretation, especially for depression-related alerts.
