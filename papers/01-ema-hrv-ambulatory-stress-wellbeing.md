## Summary

This study integrates ambulatory physiological monitoring (continuous HR/HRV) with ecological momentary assessment (EMA) to explain daily wellbeing in real-world settings. It demonstrates how combining objective biosignals with subjective reports captures stress dynamics that neither measure alone can detect.

## Key Findings

- Hourly ambulatory HRV data combined with EMA self-reports captures real-world stress dynamics
- Physiological signals and subjective reports provide complementary information about wellbeing
- Perceived control modulates the relationship between physiological stress markers and subjective wellbeing
- Real-world ambulatory monitoring reveals stress patterns invisible in laboratory settings

## MAON Applicability

Directly validates MAON's approach of combining objective wearable biosignals with user self-reports via conversation. The finding that subjective and objective measures are complementary supports MAON's multi-signal architecture.

## Methodology Notes

Published in Frontiers in Psychology (2025). Uses continuous cardiovascular monitoring with hourly EMA surveys over multiple days. Real-world ambulatory design. Open access.
