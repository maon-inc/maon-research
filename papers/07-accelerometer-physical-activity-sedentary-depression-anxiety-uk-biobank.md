## Summary

A large prospective UK Biobank study (71,556 participants) examining dose-response associations between objectively-measured physical activity, sedentary behavior, and mental health disorders using accelerometer data.

## Key Findings

- First large-scale study with objectively-measured physical activity and dose-response for mental health
- Sedentary individuals had significantly higher depression and anxiety risk
- Dose-response curve shows diminishing returns above certain activity thresholds
- Replacing sedentary time with even light physical activity reduces mental health risk
- Accelerometer-derived measures are more accurate than self-report for these associations

## MAON Applicability

MAON's Apple Watch accelerometry can replicate these accelerometer measurements. The dose-response curves provide calibration data for MAON to determine when activity levels cross from protective to concerning thresholds.

## Methodology Notes

Prospective cohort published in Am J Geriatric Psychiatry (2024). 71,556 UK Biobank participants. Accelerometer-measured activity. Dose-response analysis. Large sample.
