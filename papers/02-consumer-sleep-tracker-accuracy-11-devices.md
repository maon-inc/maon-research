## Summary

A multicenter validation study comparing 11 consumer sleep trackers against polysomnography. Provides comprehensive accuracy data across multiple device categories (wearable, nearable, airable) for sleep staging and sleep/wake detection.

## Key Findings

- Consumer sleep trackers generally overestimate total sleep time and underestimate wake time
- Sleep staging accuracy varies significantly across devices and sleep stages
- Trend detection (night-to-night changes) is more reliable than absolute stage durations
- Wrist-worn devices performed comparably to or better than nearable/airable alternatives for most metrics

## MAON Applicability

Confirms that MAON should focus on within-person trends rather than absolute sleep stage values. MAON's z-score approach (comparing to personal baseline) is well-suited given the systematic biases in consumer devices.

## Methodology Notes

Prospective multicenter validation study published in JMIR mHealth and uHealth (2023). Compared 11 consumer devices against PSG. Multiple sleep lab sites. Healthy adult participants.
