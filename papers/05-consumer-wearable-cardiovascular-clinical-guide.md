## Summary

A comprehensive guide covering consumer-grade wearable devices (smartwatches, wristbands, rings) and their applications in cardiovascular clinical care and population health research. The review introduces common wearable sensors, describes measurable health parameters, evaluates measurement validity and accuracy, and discusses practical considerations for integrating wearable data into clinical workflows.

## Key Findings

- Consumer wearables using PPG and ECG sensors can continuously and remotely monitor heart rate, HRV, blood oxygen, and rhythm abnormalities in real-world settings
- Smartwatches demonstrate high sensitivity and specificity for atrial fibrillation detection, with Apple Watch and similar devices achieving clinical-grade accuracy in many contexts
- Resting heart rate tracked via wearables serves as a meaningful population-level biomarker for cardiovascular risk stratification
- Continuous monitoring enables early detection of cardiovascular anomalies that episodic clinical visits may miss
- Data quality varies across devices and wearing conditions, requiring careful interpretation in clinical settings
- The review identifies key gaps in regulatory frameworks and standardization for wearable-derived cardiovascular data

## MAON Applicability

Directly relevant to MAON's cognitive score health domain, which uses resting heart rate and HRV from Apple Watch/Apple Health as core signals. The paper validates the approach of using consumer wearables for continuous cardiovascular monitoring and provides evidence supporting the clinical meaningfulness of the metrics MAON tracks. Also relevant to Garmin integration and any future expansion of health anomaly detection features.
