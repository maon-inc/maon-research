## Summary

A systematic review examining associations between fitness/diet tracking technology use and disordered eating behavior. Critically important for MAON's safe deployment with eating disorder populations.

## Key Findings

- Fitness tracker users report higher levels of disordered eating and compulsive exercise than non-users
- Tracking primarily for weight/shape management (vs. health/fitness) associated with highest disordered eating levels
- Qualitative evidence of unintended consequences: pressure to meet goals, guilt, compulsive monitoring
- Shift from "wanting" to "having" to exercise may indicate wearable-reinforced vulnerability
- No causal conclusions can be drawn; eating disorders have complex multi-factor etiology

## MAON Applicability

Critical safety paper for MAON. MAON must be designed to avoid reinforcing compulsive monitoring behaviors in eating disorder users. This means avoiding explicit calorie/step goals, not displaying data in ways that trigger competitive tracking, and framing wellness holistically rather than through metrics.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review published in European Eating Disorders Review (2025). Covers both quantitative and qualitative evidence. Important safety considerations for digital health apps. Open access.
