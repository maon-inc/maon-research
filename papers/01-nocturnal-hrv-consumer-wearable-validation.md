## Summary

This validation study compares nocturnal resting heart rate and HRV measurements from consumer wearables against reference-standard methods. It directly assesses the accuracy of the type of data MAON collects from Apple Watch during sleep.

## Key Findings

- Consumer wearables provide valid nocturnal resting heart rate and HRV measurements
- Nocturnal measurements show better agreement with clinical-grade devices than daytime measurements
- Sleep-period HRV collection minimizes motion artifacts and improves data quality
- Validation supports using consumer wearable overnight HRV for health monitoring applications

## MAON Applicability

Directly validates MAON's core data collection strategy of using overnight Apple Watch HRV measurements. This paper can be cited when MAON needs to justify the accuracy of its wearable-derived HRV data to users or in its pattern interpretation confidence levels.
