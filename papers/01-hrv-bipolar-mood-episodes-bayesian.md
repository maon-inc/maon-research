## Summary

This study uses Bayesian analysis to characterize HRV changes during acute manic and depressive episodes of bipolar disorder. It provides quantitative estimates of how HRV shifts between mood states, directly informing how MAON should interpret HRV patterns in bipolar users.

## Key Findings

- During manic states, HRV was increased by 18% compared to depressed states
- Manic HRV was 17% higher than euthymic (stable) states
- No significant HRV difference between depressive and euthymic states
- HRV dynamics during acute bipolar episodes are poorly understood but measurable with wearables
- Autonomic nervous system dysfunction is a feature of bipolar disorder detectable via HRV

## MAON Applicability

For users who self-report bipolar disorder, MAON can use this to interpret HRV changes: paradoxically elevated HRV may signal manic episodes rather than good health. This is critical for avoiding misinterpretation of HRV increases in bipolar users.

## Methodology Notes

Published in npj Mental Health Research (2024). Bayesian analytical approach provides probabilistic estimates. Examines HRV across distinct mood states (mania, depression, euthymia).
