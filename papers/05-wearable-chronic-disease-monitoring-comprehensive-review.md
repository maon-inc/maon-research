## Summary

A comprehensive review exploring the effectiveness and challenges of wearable health devices in chronic disease management across multiple medical fields, including cardiology, respiratory health, neurology, endocrinology, orthopedics, oncology, and mental health. Published in Cureus in September 2024, the review synthesizes evidence on how consumer and clinical-grade wearables are being deployed for continuous health monitoring beyond acute illness detection.

## Key Findings

- In cardiology, wearables have proven effective for monitoring hypertension, detecting arrhythmias, and aiding cardiac rehabilitation
- Respiratory health applications enhance asthma management and enable continuous monitoring of critical pulmonary parameters
- Neurological applications include seizure detection and Parkinson's disease management, with wearables showing promising improvement in patient outcomes
- Endocrinology applications advance thyroid dysfunction monitoring, fertility tracking, and diabetes management through continuous glucose monitors
- Mental health monitoring via wearables is an emerging application area with promising but early-stage evidence
- Key challenges include data accuracy, user compliance, integration with existing healthcare infrastructure, and privacy/security concerns

## MAON Applicability

Reinforces the broader clinical validity of the wearable health monitoring approach that MAON employs. While MAON focuses on mental health and wellness, this review demonstrates that continuous wearable monitoring is gaining traction across all chronic disease categories. The identified challenges around data accuracy and user compliance are directly relevant to MAON's design decisions around background sync, data freshness scoring, and user engagement strategies. The cross-domain evidence supports MAON's multi-signal approach (combining HRV, sleep, activity, and other metrics) as aligned with broader trends in wearable health technology.
