## Summary

Studies autonomic nervous system differences in chronic insomnia patients using wearable devices. Demonstrates increased sympathetic activity during sleep in insomnia, detectable through consumer wearable monitoring.

## Key Findings

- Chronic insomnia patients show increased sympathetic activity during sleep
- Higher nocturnal heart rate in insomnia vs. healthy sleepers
- Wearable devices (including PPG-based) can detect autonomic differences in insomnia
- Autonomic imbalance persists throughout the night in insomnia
- Multiple physiological signals (HR, HRV, respiratory patterns) contribute to insomnia detection

## MAON Applicability

For users who self-report sleep disorders, MAON can use nocturnal autonomic patterns (elevated HR, reduced HRV) as objective insomnia indicators. Changes in these patterns may signal treatment effectiveness or worsening sleep dysfunction.

## Methodology Notes

Published in Sleep Medicine (2024). Uses wearable device (Empatica E4) for autonomic monitoring. Compares insomnia patients to healthy controls. Clinically relevant findings.
