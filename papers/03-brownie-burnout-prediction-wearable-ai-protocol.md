## Summary

A decentralized digital health study protocol designed to predict burnout in registered nurses using wearable sensors and AI. Outlines a framework for prospective burnout prediction from physiological data that could generalize beyond healthcare.

## Key Findings

- Protocol combines continuous wearable physiological monitoring with validated burnout assessments
- Targets prospective prediction of burnout onset rather than retrospective detection
- Decentralized design enables real-world data collection without laboratory visits
- Identifies physiological biomarkers of burnout as primary endpoints
- AI/ML models will predict burnout trajectory from wearable-derived features

## MAON Applicability

The prospective prediction approach directly mirrors MAON's goal of detecting drift before it becomes a problem. The decentralized, wearable-only design validates MAON's architecture of remote continuous monitoring without clinical infrastructure.
