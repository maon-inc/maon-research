## Summary

The Mount Sinai Warrior Watch Study found that Apple Watch HRV changes can signal COVID-19 infection up to 7 days before diagnosis via nasal swab. HRV patterns normalized 7-14 days after diagnosis, tracking recovery.

## Key Findings

- Apple Watch HRV changes detected COVID-19 up to 7 days before positive nasal swab test
- HRV pattern began normalizing 7-14 days after diagnosis, tracking recovery timeline
- Asymptomatic individuals also showed detectable HRV changes
- Study specifically validated Apple Watch (MAON's primary device)
- Healthcare workers provided a high-exposure study population

## MAON Applicability

Directly validates using Apple Watch HRV for illness detection in MAON. The 7-day pre-symptomatic window gives MAON substantial lead time. The recovery tracking capability (HRV normalization after illness) validates MAON's recovery score model.

## Methodology Notes

Published by Mount Sinai (2021). Prospective study of healthcare workers wearing Apple Watches. Specifically validates Apple Watch HRV. Highly cited in wearable illness detection literature.
