## Summary

This study examines how sleep fragmentation (measured by wake after sleep onset) impacts next-day cognitive performance and fatigue levels. It provides evidence for the functional consequences of disrupted sleep continuity.

## Key Findings

- Sleep fragmentation, measured as minutes of wakefulness at night, predicts slower processing speed the next day
- Sleep continuity may be more relevant to daily cognitive functioning than total sleep time
- The glymphatic system (brain waste clearance) operates during deep sleep and is disrupted by fragmentation
- Wake after sleep onset (WASO) is a robust predictor of next-day cognitive and mood outcomes

## MAON Applicability

MAON detects sleep fragmentation via Apple Watch sleep staging. This paper supports using WASO and fragmentation metrics to predict next-day cognitive function and mood, informing morning check-in messages about expected energy and focus levels.

## Methodology Notes

Published in IJERPH (2022). Ambulatory assessment design with actigraphy over 16 days. Older adult sample (70+). Objective sleep measurement with next-day cognitive testing.
