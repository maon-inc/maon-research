## Summary

A UK Biobank study examining the dose-response relationship between accelerometer-measured physical activity and depression. Uses objective activity measurement to map the precise dose-response curve.

## Key Findings

- Inverse dose-response relationship between accelerometer-measured activity and depression risk
- Optimal benefits observed at approximately 10,000 steps per day
- Risk reduction plateaus at approximately 10,000 total steps, 4,800 purposeful steps, and 6,500 incidental steps
- Higher-intensity steps provide additional benefits beyond count alone
- Moderate activity around 90 min/day associated with lowest mental health burden

## MAON Applicability

Provides the most precise dose-response data for MAON's step count monitoring. MAON can use the 10,000-step plateau as an optimal target and 4,800 purposeful steps as a minimum meaningful threshold. The intensity-matters finding supports MAON distinguishing active vs. passive movement.
