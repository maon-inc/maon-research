## Summary

This recent meta-analysis examines objective sleep measures in adults with and without depression across different age groups, providing nuanced findings about which sleep metrics are most reliable biomarkers of depression.

## Key Findings

- All adults with depression had less total sleep time, delayed sleep onset latency, higher wake after sleep onset, shorter REM latency, and greater REM density than controls
- REM latency and REM density are not consistent markers of depression across all age groups
- Age modulates the relationship between sleep architecture and depression
- Sleep continuity measures (WASO, sleep efficiency) may be more robust biomarkers than stage-specific measures

## MAON Applicability

MAON should prioritize sleep continuity metrics (WASO, sleep efficiency, total sleep time) as depression indicators, as these are more consistent across age groups than REM-specific metrics. MAON should factor in user age when interpreting sleep stage data.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review and meta-analysis published in Sleep Medicine Reviews (2024). Distinguishes between adult and older adult populations. Provides age-stratified effect sizes.
