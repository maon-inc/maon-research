## Summary

This multicenter randomized controlled trial evaluated ELME, a chatbot that guided participants through three weeks of structured training on stress management, mindfulness, and interoception. The intervention was delivered through two daily interactive sessions via smartphone (approximately 10-20 minutes each), representing a proactive, push-based approach to mental health support. The study included 118 individuals with medium to high stress levels randomized to intervention (n=59) or treatment-as-usual control (n=59).

## Key Findings

- The chatbot delivered proactive, scheduled intervention sessions twice daily, providing structured psychoeducation and practical exercises
- Ecological momentary assessments (EMA) of stress and interoceptive sensibility were collected during both conditions, enabling real-time tracking
- Primary outcome was perceived stress, with secondary outcomes including mindfulness, interoception, subjective well-being, and emotion regulation
- Outcomes were assessed at three timepoints: pre-intervention, post-intervention (3 weeks), and follow-up (6 weeks)
- The study demonstrates feasibility of push-based chatbot interventions that proactively engage users rather than waiting for user-initiated contact
- The combination of psychoeducation with practical mindfulness and interoception exercises represents a structured approach to daily stress management

## MAON Applicability

Highly relevant to MAON's proactive agent cron system, which reaches out to users with health-related messages and interventions. The ELME approach of scheduled, twice-daily interactive sessions mirrors MAON's proactive check-in model. The ecological momentary assessment methodology aligns with how MAON collects continuous physiological data from wearables to contextualize interventions. The stress, mindfulness, and interoception focus areas overlap with MAON's breathing activity feature and HRV-based stress monitoring.

## Methodology Notes

Multicenter two-armed RCT with 118 participants (medium to high stress levels per Perceived Stress Scale). Three-week intervention period with 6-week follow-up. Ecological momentary assessment methodology used for real-time data collection. Published in JMIR Mental Health, a well-regarded digital health journal.
