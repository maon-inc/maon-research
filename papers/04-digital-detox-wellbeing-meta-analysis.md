## Summary

A systematic review and meta-analysis of 26 studies (18 included in meta-analysis) with 8,147 participants examining the effectiveness of digital detoxification interventions on well-being among social media users. Found that digital detox significantly improved both subjective well-being and psychological well-being, providing quantitative support for screen time reduction strategies.

## Key Findings

- Digital detoxification significantly improved subjective well-being (SMD = 0.21, 95% CI: 0.06-0.34, p < 0.01)
- Psychological well-being also improved significantly (SMD = 0.27, 95% CI: 0.09-0.46, p < 0.05)
- 26 eligible studies identified from comprehensive database searches (PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, ProQuest)
- 18 studies with 8,147 participants included in the meta-analysis
- Reducing rather than completely abstaining from social media showed more consistent benefits
- Effect sizes were small to medium, suggesting meaningful but modest improvements

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review and meta-analysis published in Cyberpsychology, Behavior, and Social Networking (2024). Searched PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, ProQuest, and Google Scholar up to November 2023. 26 studies met inclusion criteria; 18 with sufficient data for meta-analysis. Random-effects models used. Assessed publication bias and heterogeneity.
