## Summary

A systematic review and meta-analysis of 18 studies (1,352 participants) evaluating the efficacy of remote heart rate variability biofeedback (HRV-B) interventions for mental health outcomes. Found medium effect sizes for improving both depression and HRV compared to control conditions, supporting the viability of remotely delivered HRV biofeedback as a scalable mental health intervention.

## Key Findings

- Remote HRV biofeedback showed a medium effect size for reducing depression symptoms compared to control conditions
- Significant improvements in resting HRV were observed across intervention groups, indicating improved autonomic regulation
- Remote delivery via smartphones and wearable heart rate monitors was feasible and maintained effectiveness comparable to in-person protocols
- Most protocols used resonance frequency breathing at approximately 6 breaths per minute
- Daily sessions of 10-20 minutes were the most common duration across successful interventions
- Heterogeneity in methodology (devices, session length, biofeedback modality) limits cross-study comparisons

## MAON Applicability

Directly supports MAON's breathing intervention features. MAON already collects HRV data from Apple Watch and Garmin and offers guided breathing sessions. This meta-analysis validates the approach of using remote, wearable-monitored HRV biofeedback for mental health improvement. MAON could implement resonance frequency breathing protocols and track HRV changes over time to demonstrate intervention effectiveness to users.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review and meta-analysis of 18 studies with 1,352 total participants. Published in Applied Psychophysiology and Biofeedback (2025). Searched multiple databases. Included studies using remote or portable HRV biofeedback with mental health outcomes. Quality assessment performed on all included studies.
