## Summary

Proof-of-concept study examining interoception as a mechanism through which mobile HRV biofeedback reduces eating disorder symptoms. Building on a controlled trial that demonstrated HRV biofeedback successfully enhances adaptive interoception, this secondary analysis explored how an mHealth app delivering resonance-frequency breathing training impacts the interoceptive pathways implicated in disordered eating.

## Key Findings

- Mobile HRV biofeedback improved interoceptive awareness, which mediated reductions in eating disorder symptom severity
- Resonance-frequency breathing delivered through a smartphone app was feasible and acceptable for individuals with eating disorder symptoms
- Interoceptive deficits are a core feature of eating disorders, and HRV biofeedback directly targets this mechanism by enhancing body-signal awareness
- The mobile delivery format enabled practice outside clinical settings, increasing intervention accessibility and dose
- Autonomic regulation improvements (indexed by HRV changes) paralleled improvements in self-reported eating pathology
- The approach bridges physiological intervention (breathing/HRV) with psychological mechanisms (interoception) relevant to eating disorder maintenance

## MAON Applicability

Connects two core MAON features: the breathing exercise module (4-7-8 technique) and HRV monitoring from Apple Watch/Garmin. This paper suggests that MAON's breathing interventions could be positioned not just as stress reduction tools but as interoceptive training that may benefit users with disordered eating patterns. The finding that mobile-delivered HRV biofeedback improves interoception supports MAON's approach of combining wearable physiological data with guided interventions.

## Methodology Notes

Secondary analysis of a proof-of-concept study. Participants used a mobile HRV biofeedback app for guided resonance-frequency breathing. Measures included interoceptive awareness questionnaires and eating disorder symptom scales. Limitations include small sample size typical of proof-of-concept designs and the need for larger RCTs to confirm mediation pathways.
