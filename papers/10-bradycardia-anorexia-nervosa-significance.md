## Summary

Examines the clinical significance of bradycardia (abnormally low heart rate) in anorexia nervosa. Bradycardia affects up to 95% of anorexia patients and results from increased vagal tone and decreased metabolism.

## Key Findings

- Bradycardia affects up to 95% of individuals with anorexia nervosa
- Mechanism: increased vagal tone and decreased metabolism from low caloric intake
- Heart rate can drop to dangerously low levels (below 40 bpm in severe cases)
- Nutritional rehabilitation and weight restoration reverse bradycardia
- Bradycardia severity correlates with degree of malnutrition

## MAON Applicability

MAON's continuous HR monitoring can detect bradycardia patterns in users who self-report eating disorders. A sustained resting heart rate below 50 bpm in a non-athletic user could be flagged as a concern. Conversely, heart rate normalization during recovery can be tracked as a positive indicator.
