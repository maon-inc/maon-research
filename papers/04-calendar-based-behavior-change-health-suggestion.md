## Summary

This paper presents Tweak, a cloud-based health promotion system that integrates tailored, context-aware health suggestions directly into users' digital work calendars. Through two four-week field studies with 21 participants, the researchers explored how calendar-based delivery of behavior change interventions could stimulate healthy behaviors in office workers.

## Key Findings

- Calendar integration for health suggestions stimulated reflective behavior and curiosity about one's own schedule
- Users found the calendar-based delivery mechanism convenient and easy to use
- The system adapted to changing user profiles and used personal and contextual data to situate suggestions appropriately
- Schedule density and free time windows were key contextual factors for timing health suggestions
- Embedding health interventions within existing digital workflows (calendars) reduced friction compared to standalone health apps
- The approach demonstrates how digital calendars can serve as a delivery channel for just-in-time behavior change interventions

## MAON Applicability

Directly relevant to MAON's calendar blocking and smart schedule features. The concept of using calendar context to trigger wellbeing interventions mirrors MAON's approach of blocking distracting apps during calendar events and providing AI-driven schedule management. Validates the idea that calendar data is a meaningful signal for digital wellbeing interventions, supporting MAON's calendar domain in the cognitive score.

## Methodology Notes

Two four-week field studies with a combined sample of 21 office workers. Qualitative and quantitative evaluation of user engagement and experience. Published at ECCE 2021, a reputable HCI conference. Small sample size limits statistical power but provides rich qualitative insights into user experience with calendar-based health interventions.
