## Summary

A comprehensive review framing ADHD as a circadian rhythm disorder, with evidence that sleep disturbances affect up to 80% of adults with ADHD. It documents the delayed sleep-wake timing, evening chronotype predominance, and melatonin onset delays characteristic of ADHD.

## Key Findings

- Sleep disturbances affect up to 80% of adults and 82% of children with ADHD
- Delayed sleep-wake timing occurs in up to 78% of individuals with ADHD
- Dim-light melatonin onset (DLMO) is delayed by ~45 min in children and ~90 min in adults with ADHD
- Evening chronotype predominance is a robust finding across ADHD studies
- Children with ADHD spend more time in NREM Stage 1 (lighter, more fragmented sleep)

## MAON Applicability

For ADHD users, MAON should expect later sleep timing and use circadian-adjusted baselines. Late bedtimes and irregular sleep schedules in ADHD users are partly neurobiological (not just behavioral), so MAON's messaging should be informative rather than prescriptive about sleep timing.

## Methodology Notes

Comprehensive systematic review published in Frontiers in Psychiatry (2025). Covers children, adolescents, and adults with ADHD. Integrates polysomnographic, actigraphic, and melatonin data. Open access.
