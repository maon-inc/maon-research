## Summary

A systematic review in The Lancet Digital Health evaluating wearable sensor performance for SARS-CoV-2 detection. Covers multiple device types, signal modalities, and ML approaches across 9+ studies.

## Key Findings

- HR, HRV, respiratory rate, skin temperature, and activity are the most commonly reported detection parameters
- 88% of COVID-19 cases detected with anomaly models (median 4 days pre-symptomatic)
- Alternative models detected 56% of cases median 6.94 days before symptoms
- 9 studies used ML including anomaly detection autoencoders, gradient-boosted classifiers, and neural networks
- Multi-signal approaches (combining HR + temperature + activity) outperform single-signal

## MAON Applicability

Validates MAON's multi-signal anomaly detection approach for illness. The combination of signals MAON collects (HR, HRV, respiratory rate, wrist temperature, activity) maps perfectly to the most effective detection frameworks described here. The 4-day median pre-symptomatic detection provides a realistic lead-time expectation.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review published in The Lancet Digital Health (2022). Covers multiple wearable devices and ML approaches. High-quality evidence from a top-tier journal. Includes both prospective and retrospective studies.
