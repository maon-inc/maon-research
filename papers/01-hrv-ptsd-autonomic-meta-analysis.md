## Summary

This meta-analysis quantifies autonomic dysfunction in PTSD as indexed by HRV. It demonstrates that individuals with PTSD have significantly lower HRV than healthy controls, reflecting reduced parasympathetic (vagal) activity and autonomic inflexibility.

## Key Findings

- PTSD is associated with significantly reduced HRV compared to healthy controls at both rest and during stress
- Reduced parasympathetic (vagal) activity is a hallmark of PTSD autonomic profiles
- The autonomic dysregulation indicates a general ANS imbalance, not just stress reactivity
- HRV reductions in PTSD are among the strongest effect sizes across psychiatric disorders

## MAON Applicability

For users who self-report PTSD, MAON should expect and account for baseline-lower HRV. This paper helps MAON calibrate its z-score thresholds for PTSD users and understand that HRV improvements may signal recovery or effective treatment engagement.

## Methodology Notes

Meta-analysis published in Psychological Medicine (2013). While older, this is a highly cited foundational paper (>500 citations) in the HRV-PTSD literature. Includes both resting and stress-condition measurements.
