## Summary

A large-scale population-based study (N=1,290 young adults, ages 33-35) examining how digital metrics from the Oura Ring differ by severity of depression and anxiety symptoms. Participants wore the ring for two weeks while mental health was assessed using validated scales (GAD-7 and Hopkins Symptom Checklist-25). The study found that depression and anxiety severity manifests in multiple physiological and behavioral channels captured by consumer wearables.

## Key Findings

- Participants with higher depression and/or anxiety symptoms had reduced heart rate variability (HRV) and elevated nocturnal heart rate compared to non-symptomatic individuals
- Depression and anxiety severity were associated with lower levels of rapid eye movement (REM) sleep
- Higher symptom severity correlated with increased deep and light sleep proportions, suggesting altered sleep architecture
- Participants with more severe symptoms showed lower daytime movement and physical activity levels
- Multiple physiological channels (cardiac, sleep, activity) simultaneously reflect mental health symptom severity, supporting multi-metric composite scoring
- Consumer-grade wearable rings can detect clinically meaningful physiological differences associated with depression and anxiety at the population level

## MAON Applicability

Highly relevant to MAON's cognitive score algorithm, which integrates HRV, resting heart rate, sleep metrics, and step counts into a composite wellness score. This study provides direct evidence that exactly these metrics (as measured by consumer wearables) differ meaningfully across depression and anxiety severity levels. The multi-metric approach validates MAON's composite scoring strategy. The finding that lower daytime movement accompanies mental health symptoms supports MAON's inclusion of step counts in its cognitive score calculation.

## Methodology Notes

Population-based cross-sectional study with N=1,290 participants (ages 33-35) from a young adult cohort. Mental health assessed via GAD-7 (anxiety) and Hopkins Symptom Checklist-25 (depression). Physiological data collected via Oura Ring over a 2-week wear period, capturing sleep architecture, nocturnal HR, HRV, and daytime movement intensity. Preprint on medRxiv (2026); not yet peer-reviewed.
