## Summary

A detailed review of REM sleep dysregulation as a core feature of depression. Documents shortened REM latency, increased REM density, and prolonged first REM period as the most consistent biological markers of depression.

## Key Findings

- Shortened REM latency is one of the most replicated biological findings in depression
- Increased REM density (frequency of rapid eye movements) distinguishes depressed patients from controls
- REM sleep alterations may precede clinical expression of depression (predictive value)
- REM sleep abnormalities persist during remission, suggesting they are trait rather than state markers
- Antidepressant medications typically suppress REM sleep, which correlates with treatment response

## MAON Applicability

MAON's Apple Watch tracks REM sleep duration and timing. While absolute REM staging accuracy is moderate, MAON can detect sustained shifts in REM proportion and timing as potential depression signals, especially when combined with other indicators.

## Methodology Notes

Narrative review published in Sleep Medicine Reviews (2013). Highly cited foundational paper on REM-depression relationship. Primarily based on polysomnographic research. Pre-dates consumer wearable era but findings remain relevant.
