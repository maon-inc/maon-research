## Summary

A state-of-the-art review published in Pediatrics summarizing 139 articles (2013-2023) on reducing or abstaining from digital media use and its effects on well-being. Highlights that there is no consistent definition of "digital detox" and that reducing rather than eliminating screen time tends to produce more beneficial outcomes. Aimed at informing parents, educators, and policymakers.

## Key Findings

- No clear or consistent definition of "digital detox" exists across the literature
- Reducing social media and smartphone time (rather than total abstinence) showed more beneficial effects on well-being
- Intervention effectiveness varies depending on the type of outcome measured (psychological, social, cognitive)
- Effects are moderated by gender, age, and contextual factors
- 139 articles reviewed spanning a decade of research (2013-2023)
- Highlights the need for standardized intervention protocols and outcome measures

## Methodology Notes

State-of-the-art narrative review published in Pediatrics (2024). Reviewed 139 articles from 2013 to 2023. Covered intervention studies, observational research, and policy recommendations. Focused on youth populations but findings generalize to young adults.
