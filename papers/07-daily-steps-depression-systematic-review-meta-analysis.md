## Summary

A systematic review and meta-analysis examining the dose-response relationship between daily step count and depression in adults. Identifies specific step thresholds associated with protective effects.

## Key Findings

- 3,500-6,999 steps/day provides protective effect against depressive symptoms
- 7,000+ steps/day provides the greatest protection against depression
- Every 1,000-step increment associated with fewer depressive symptoms
- Optimal protection plateaus around 10,000 steps/day
- Higher-intensity steps provide additional benefits beyond count alone

## MAON Applicability

Directly informs MAON's step count monitoring. MAON can use the 7,000-step threshold as a benchmark and flag when daily steps consistently fall below 3,500 as a depression risk indicator. The 1,000-step increment finding supports MAON nudging users toward small increases.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review and meta-analysis (2024). Includes studies using pedometers and accelerometers. Dose-response analysis with specific thresholds. High clinical relevance.
