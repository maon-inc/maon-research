## Summary

A prospective cohort study demonstrating that sleep regularity is a stronger predictor of all-cause mortality risk than sleep duration. Uses accelerometer-derived sleep regularity index to quantify timing consistency.

## Key Findings

- Sleep regularity predicts all-cause mortality more strongly than sleep duration
- The Sleep Regularity Index (SRI) quantifies day-to-day consistency of sleep-wake timing
- Irregular sleepers face elevated mortality risk independent of total sleep duration
- Accelerometer-derived SRI is a valid and reliable measure from consumer wearables
- Sleep regularity is modifiable, making it an actionable intervention target

## MAON Applicability

Elevates sleep regularity as a priority metric for MAON. MAON should compute and track a Sleep Regularity Index from Apple Watch data and weight it at least as heavily as sleep duration in its sleep quality score.

## Methodology Notes

Prospective cohort study published in SLEEP (2024). Large sample from UK Biobank. Uses wrist accelerometry. Long follow-up period. High-impact finding.
