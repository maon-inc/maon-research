## Summary

A feasibility study (n=37) investigated passively collected GPS-based mobility phenotypes from personal smartphones over 4 weeks in older adults. Location diversity and activity levels were associated with better cognition, less depression, and more community participation, validating mobility-based digital phenotyping as a meaningful approach for capturing everyday functioning and mood unobtrusively.

## Key Findings

- Greater activity levels and location diversity correlated with better cognitive performance and less depression
- Higher location diversity was associated with less functional impairment and more community participation
- Contrary to expectations, greater intraindividual variability (less routine) in mobility was associated with positive outcomes
- GPS-based digital phenotypes were feasibly captured from personal smartphones with high acceptability (92% retention)
- Mobility phenotypes correlated with both objective cognitive tests and subjective well-being measures
- The open-source mindLAMP app successfully collected continuous GPS trajectories with minimal user burden

## MAON Applicability

Directly relevant to MAON's location domain in the cognitive score. The app tracks location visits and uses them as one of four signals. This study validates the approach of using passively collected location/mobility data as a proxy for well-being. The finding that location diversity predicts mood supports MAON's scoring of location-based behavioral patterns. The use of passive smartphone GPS aligns with MAON's background location tracking feature.

## Methodology Notes

Prospective feasibility study with 37 adults aged 63-85 (31 cognitively healthy, 5 MCI, 1 mild dementia) using the open-source mindLAMP smartphone app for 4 weeks of passive GPS collection. Validated against cognitive tests, functional assessments, mood measures (GDS), and community participation scales. Conducted between April 2022 and January 2024. Small sample limits generalizability, but the study provides important proof-of-concept for mobility-based digital phenotyping.
