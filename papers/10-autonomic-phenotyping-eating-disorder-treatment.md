## Summary

This review article proposes integrating autonomic nervous system phenotyping and balancing interventions into eating disorder (ED) treatment. By measuring heart rate, HRV, and electrodermal activity with wearable sensor technology, clinicians can observe state and trait adaptive responses of the parasympathetic and sympathetic nervous systems, leading to more personalized treatment approaches.

## Key Findings

- Wearable sensors measuring HR, HRV, and EDA can capture both state and trait autonomic responses relevant to eating disorders
- Individuals with anorexia and atypical anorexia nervosa often present with elevated HRV, suggesting parasympathetic dominance
- For these patients, enhancing sympathetic response while reducing parasympathetic response may be therapeutic
- Binge eating and loss-of-control eating are associated with lower resting HRV
- Autonomic phenotyping allows clinicians to tailor interventions based on individual autonomic profiles
- Wearable-based autonomic monitoring could enable just-in-time interventions to disrupt problematic ED behaviors
