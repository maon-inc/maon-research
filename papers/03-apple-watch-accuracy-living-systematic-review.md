## Summary

A living systematic review and meta-analysis evaluating the accuracy of Apple Watch measurements across multiple health metrics including heart rate, HRV, sleep staging, and physical activity. This is the most comprehensive Apple Watch validation paper available.

## Key Findings

- Apple Watch HR measurements show high accuracy compared to reference devices across most conditions
- HRV accuracy is good during rest/sleep but degrades during physical activity
- Sleep staging shows moderate accuracy with systematic biases in deep sleep estimation
- Step counting is generally accurate but varies with activity type
- SpO2 measurements show acceptable accuracy for wellness monitoring
- Motion artifacts remain the primary source of measurement error

## MAON Applicability

Critical reference paper for MAON since Apple Watch is the primary data source. Informs confidence levels for each signal MAON uses and helps calibrate when measurements are most/least reliable (nocturnal > daytime, rest > activity).

## Methodology Notes

Living systematic review and meta-analysis published in npj Digital Medicine (2025). Continuously updated. Covers multiple Apple Watch generations. Includes diverse populations and measurement conditions. High-quality evidence.
