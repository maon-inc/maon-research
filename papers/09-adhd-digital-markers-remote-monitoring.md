## Summary

This prospective observational study used the ADHD Remote Technology (ART) system built on the RADAR-base platform to identify digital behavioral markers of ADHD in adults. Passive sensor data from smartphones and wearable devices were collected alongside clinical symptom questionnaires to explore which digital signals distinguish ADHD-related behavioral patterns.

## Key Findings

- Participants with ADHD were slower and more variable in response speed to smartphone notifications
- Higher variability in time intervals between questionnaire completions was observed in ADHD participants
- ADHD participants had higher daily mean response times to social and communication app notifications
- Greater changes in ambient light levels during active smartphone use were noted in the ADHD group
- These digital signals capture restlessness, inconsistent attention, and difficulties completing tasks
- The RADAR-base platform proved feasible for continuous remote monitoring of ADHD-related behaviors

## MAON Applicability

Relevant to MAON's notification and screen time monitoring features. The finding that notification response patterns serve as digital markers for ADHD connects directly to MAON's screen time tracking and notification awareness capabilities. The ambient light and smartphone usage variability metrics could inform future passive sensing features for users with ADHD who want to monitor their attention patterns.

## Methodology Notes

Prospective observational study using the RADAR-base Passive App and wearable devices for continuous background data collection, combined with an Active App for clinical questionnaire administration. Multi-site study with participants from the UK. Built on an established digital phenotyping research platform with validated data collection infrastructure.
