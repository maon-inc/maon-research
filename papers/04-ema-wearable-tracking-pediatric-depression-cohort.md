## Summary

A cohort study published in JMIR Formative Research (2025) combining smartphone-based ecological momentary assessment (EMA) with wearable activity tracking in pediatric depression. The study examined depression symptom variability, diurnal mood patterns, sleep characteristics, and physical activity in youth with major depressive disorder (MDD) compared to healthy controls, demonstrating high feasibility of combined digital monitoring approaches.

## Key Findings

- Overall EMA compliance rates were high (91.57%), demonstrating strong feasibility of smartphone-based monitoring in youth
- MDD participants showed greater symptom severity and variability over time compared with controls (beta=34.48, P<.001)
- Individuals with MDD exhibited greater diurnal variation (beta=-2.54, P<.001) with worse mood in the morning
- Life stress was a significant predictor of more severe EMA scores (beta=24.50, P<.001)
- MDD cases showed more inconsistent sleep patterns (beta=32.14, P<.001), shorter total sleep times (beta=-94.38, P<.001), and higher nap frequency (beta=14.05, P<.001)
- Wearable activity data complemented self-reported EMA to provide a more complete picture of daily functioning

## MAON Applicability

Supports MAON's combined approach of integrating wearable health data (Apple Health sleep, activity, HRV) with contextual signals (screen time, calendar, location) for mental health monitoring. The high EMA compliance rate validates the feasibility of regular self-monitoring via smartphone, relevant to MAON's proactive check-in approach via the chat agent. The diurnal mood variation findings could inform MAON's timing of AI summaries and interventions (e.g., avoiding morning check-ins for users showing worse morning mood). The sleep pattern findings reinforce the importance of MAON's sleep tracking in its cognitive score.

## Methodology Notes

Prospective cohort study comparing youth with MDD to healthy controls. Used smartphone EMA (multiple daily assessments) combined with continuous wearable activity tracking. Linear mixed-effects models for statistical analysis. Published in JMIR Formative Research (2025). High compliance rates strengthen the ecological validity of findings.
