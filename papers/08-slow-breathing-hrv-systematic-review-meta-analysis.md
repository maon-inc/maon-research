## Summary

A systematic review and meta-analysis examining the effects of voluntary slow breathing on heart rate and HRV. Confirms that slow breathing increases vagally-mediated HRV during and after sessions.

## Key Findings

- Voluntary slow breathing increases vagally-mediated HRV during breathing sessions
- Effects persist immediately after training and after multi-session interventions
- Optimal breathing rate of 4.5-6.5 breaths per minute (resonance frequency)
- 23 of 30 studies showed significant improvements in at least one HRV parameter (RMSSD, HF-HRV, LF/HF)
- 20 minutes daily resonance breathing for 4 weeks improves parasympathetic activity

## MAON Applicability

MAON could recommend slow breathing exercises as an evidence-based intervention when detecting elevated stress signals. MAON can track respiratory rate to verify if a user's baseline rate decreases over time as they practice breathing techniques, validating intervention effectiveness.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review and meta-analysis published in Journal of Psychosomatic Research (2022). 30 studies included. Covers multiple breathing protocols. Quantifies HRV improvements.
