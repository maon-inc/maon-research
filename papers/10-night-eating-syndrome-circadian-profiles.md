## Summary

Examines circadian rhythm profiles in women with night eating syndrome (NES). Identifies specific neuroendocrine phase delays and amplitude reductions that characterize the syndrome.

## Key Findings

- NES patients show delayed circadian food intake but normal sleep-wake cycle (dissociation)
- Phase delays of 1.0-2.8 hours in leptin and insulin rhythms
- Melatonin rhythm also phase-delayed
- Ghrelin-leptin synchronization disrupted by approximately 6 hours
- Reduced amplitudes in cortisol, ghrelin, and insulin circadian rhythms
- The ghrelin-leptin mismatch may represent a physiological marker for NES

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks sleep timing and can detect late-night activity patterns (screen use, movement) that may indicate nocturnal eating behavior. Combined with disrupted sleep architecture and circadian phase shifts, MAON could flag NES-consistent patterns.

## Methodology Notes

Published in Journal of Biological Rhythms (2009). Detailed circadian profiling with multiple hormone measurements. Small sample but highly detailed. Well-cited foundational paper.
