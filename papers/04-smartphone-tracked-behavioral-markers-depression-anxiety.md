## Summary

A cross-sectional digital phenotyping study using smartphone-tracked behavioral markers (app usage, screen time, location, activity) to recognize depression and anxiety symptoms. One of the most recent studies directly validating MAON's approach.

## Key Findings

- Smartphone-tracked behavioral markers can distinguish individuals with depression and anxiety symptoms
- App usage patterns, screen time, and location data are predictive features
- App switching frequency and session fragmentation serve as digital biomarkers
- Passive tracking requires no user effort, enabling continuous monitoring
- Machine learning models achieve clinically meaningful prediction accuracy

## MAON Applicability

Directly validates MAON's behavioral signal layer. The specific features used (app usage, screen time, app switching) are exactly what MAON tracks. This is among the most recent papers validating the digital phenotyping approach for depression and anxiety recognition.

## Methodology Notes

Published in JMIR Mental Health (2026). Cross-sectional design. Uses passively tracked smartphone behavioral data. Recent publication with current methodology.
