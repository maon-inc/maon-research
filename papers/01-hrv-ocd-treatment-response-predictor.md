## Summary

Investigates HRV differences between OCD patients and healthy controls, and evaluates whether baseline HRV predicts treatment response. Found reduced HRV in medicated OCD patients, with HRV metrics potentially distinguishing treatment responders from non-responders.

## Key Findings

- OCD patients showed higher resting heart rate and lower HRV compared to healthy controls
- Only medicated patients displayed significantly lower HRV vs controls; unmedicated patients showed HR alterations but preserved HRV
- HRV parameters may help separate therapy responders from non-responders
- Findings suggest medication effects interact with autonomic dysfunction in OCD
- Baseline vagal tone may predict treatment trajectory

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks HRV continuously. For users who self-report OCD, reduced HRV (especially RMSSD and SDNN) may reflect OCD severity or medication effects. MAON could track HRV trends during treatment as a complementary monitoring signal.

## Methodology Notes

Published in Clinical Neurophysiology (2022). Compared OCD patients (medicated and unmedicated subgroups) to healthy controls. Standard ECG-derived HRV metrics. Evaluated treatment prediction utility.
