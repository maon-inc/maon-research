## Summary

The first multi-institutional, longitudinal, preregistered randomized controlled trial testing a generative AI-powered mobile app ("Flourish") for proactive mental health support. Over six weeks in Fall 2024, 486 undergraduate students across three U.S. institutions were randomized to app access or waitlist control, with the app delivering daily personalized AI-driven check-ins and support without waiting for user initiation.

## Key Findings

- Participants in the treatment condition reported significantly greater positive affect compared to control
- Treatment group showed increased resilience over the six-week period
- Social well-being improved, including increased belonging, closeness to community, and reduced loneliness
- Treatment participants were buffered against declines in mindfulness and flourishing that occurred in the control group
- The proactive design (AI initiates contact rather than waiting for user) represents a shift from reactive to preventive mental health support
- Effects were observed at a population level among non-clinical undergraduate students, suggesting utility for prevention rather than just treatment

## MAON Applicability

Directly relevant to MAON's proactive agent cron feature, which sends unprompted AI messages to users based on their health data and behavioral patterns. This study provides the first RCT evidence that proactive AI-initiated mental health support can improve wellbeing outcomes, validating MAON's approach of having its AI agent reach out to users rather than waiting for them to seek help. The positive effects on resilience and loneliness reduction align with MAON's goals as a wellness companion.

## Methodology Notes

Preregistered multi-site RCT with 486 undergraduate students from Foothill College, Chapman University, and University of Washington. Randomized to app access vs. waitlist control over 6 weeks. Measured positive affect, resilience, social well-being, mindfulness, and flourishing. Published as a Harvard Business School working paper (not yet peer-reviewed in a traditional journal).
