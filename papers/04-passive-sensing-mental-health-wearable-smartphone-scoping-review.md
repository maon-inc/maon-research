## Summary

Scoping review of passive sensing approaches for mental health monitoring using ML with wearables and smartphones. Covers 66+ studies on digital phenotyping using non-invasive signals from consumer devices for depression, anxiety, stress, and bipolar disorder prediction.

## Key Findings

- 66 peer-reviewed studies identified using passive signals from wearables/smartphones for mental health monitoring
- Most commonly collected data: affect/emotions/mood (71% of studies) and behaviors like activity/diet (71%)
- Physiological data (sleep, HR, HRV, activity) collected passively for minimum 3 days in most studies
- Heart rate and step count are the most effective biomarkers for depression detection
- Sleep and social interaction patterns are strongest for anxiety detection
- Methodological heterogeneity remains a major challenge for clinical translation

## MAON Applicability

Comprehensive overview of the exact approach MAON takes. Validates MAON's passive sensing architecture and highlights which signal combinations are most effective for different conditions. Informs MAON's weighting of HR/steps for depression monitoring and sleep/social patterns for anxiety monitoring.

## Methodology Notes

Published in JMIR (2025). Scoping review covering multiple databases through 2024. Evaluated ML approaches and passive sensing modalities. Open access.
