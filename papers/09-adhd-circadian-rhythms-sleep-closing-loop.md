## Summary

Comprehensive review examining the interplay between circadian rhythms, sleep disturbances, and ADHD. Proposes that circadian rhythm disruption is a core feature of ADHD rather than a mere comorbidity.

## Key Findings

- Circadian rhythm disruption (delayed phase) is a core feature of ADHD
- Delayed melatonin onset is prevalent in ADHD populations
- Sleep-onset insomnia in ADHD is often secondary to circadian phase delay
- Treating circadian misalignment may improve ADHD symptoms
- Evening chronotype is overrepresented in ADHD populations

## MAON Applicability

Positions circadian monitoring as especially important for ADHD users. MAON should track sleep timing patterns and detect circadian phase shifts in ADHD users, which may signal symptom changes or treatment response.

## Methodology Notes

Book chapter/review from Sleep-Wake Neurobiology and Pharmacology (2019). Comprehensive coverage of ADHD-circadian-sleep relationships. Synthesizes clinical and basic science evidence.
