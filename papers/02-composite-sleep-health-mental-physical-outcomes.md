## Summary

This study validates a composite sleep health score incorporating regularity, satisfaction, alertness, timing, efficiency, and duration (RU SATED framework). It demonstrates that multidimensional sleep assessment predicts mental health outcomes better than single metrics.

## Key Findings

- Greater composite sleep health was associated with lower risk in emotional, cognitive, and social domains
- Composite scores predicted reduced odds for current mood or anxiety disorders
- The multidimensional approach explained additional variance beyond individual sleep variables
- Sleep regularity, satisfaction, alertness, timing, efficiency, and duration each contribute independently

## MAON Applicability

Validates MAON's multi-component sleep quality scoring approach. MAON should compute a composite sleep health score incorporating multiple dimensions (duration, efficiency, architecture, regularity, timing) rather than relying on any single metric.

## Methodology Notes

Cross-sectional study published in Sleep Health (2019). Adolescent sample prone to eveningness. Uses the RU SATED multidimensional sleep health framework. Open access via PMC.
