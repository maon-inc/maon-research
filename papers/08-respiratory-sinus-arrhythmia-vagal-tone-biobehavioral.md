## Summary

Examines respiratory sinus arrhythmia (RSA) as a measure of vagal tone and its role in emotional regulation and biobehavioral integration. Provides updated understanding of how breathing-heart rate coupling relates to psychological function.

## Key Findings

- Elevated RSA is associated with better emotional regulation, social engagement, and physiological resilience
- RSA serves as a bridge between vagal activity and cognitive flexibility/emotion regulation
- Respiratory parameters can confound RSA interpretation, requiring careful methodology
- Interindividual RSA-vagal tone associations are more modest than intraindividual ones
- RSA measurement is influenced by physical activity level, requiring context-aware interpretation

## MAON Applicability

MAON collects both respiratory rate and HRV, which together can approximate RSA-like metrics. The caveats about activity-level confounding support MAON's focus on nocturnal measurements when both signals are most reliable.
