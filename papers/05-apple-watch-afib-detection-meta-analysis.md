## Summary

A systematic review and meta-analysis evaluating the diagnostic accuracy of the Apple Watch electrocardiogram for detecting atrial fibrillation. The study pooled data from 11 studies comprising 4,241 participants to quantify sensitivity and specificity of Apple Watch ECG for AF detection.

## Key Findings

- Pooled sensitivity of Apple Watch for AF detection was 94.8% (95% CI: 91.7-96.8%)
- Pooled specificity was 95.0% (95% CI: 88.6-97.8%)
- Apple Watch ECG achieved clinical-grade diagnostic accuracy comparable to traditional screening methods
- Manual interpretation by trained personnel achieved higher sensitivity (96%) than the automated algorithm (86%), though specificity was similar
- All tested consumer devices achieved 100% sensitivity for AF detection when considering the first good-quality recording
- Results support consumer wearables as viable screening tools for cardiac arrhythmias in ambulatory settings

## MAON Applicability

Relevant to MAON's health monitoring capabilities through Apple Watch integration. While MAON currently focuses on resting heart rate and HRV, this paper validates the broader diagnostic potential of the Apple Watch hardware that MAON users already wear. Supports the credibility of Apple Watch as a medical-grade sensing platform and could inform future feature development around cardiac rhythm monitoring or anomaly alerts.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review and meta-analysis following PRISMA guidelines. Included 11 studies with 4,241 total participants. Searched PubMed, Embase, and Cochrane Library. Used bivariate random-effects model for pooled estimates. Published in JACC: Advances (American College of Cardiology).
