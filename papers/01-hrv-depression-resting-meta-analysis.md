## Summary

This systematic review and meta-analysis specifically examines resting-state HRV in adults with depression compared to healthy controls. It provides quantitative estimates of HRV reduction in depression, directly relevant to MAON's overnight and resting HRV measurements.

## Key Findings

- Depressed patients show significantly reduced resting HRV compared to healthy controls
- Both time-domain (SDNN, RMSSD) and frequency-domain measures are affected
- The reduction reflects autonomic dysregulation with sympathetic dominance and parasympathetic withdrawal
- Major depressive disorder is associated with blunted autonomic flexibility at rest

## MAON Applicability

MAON collects overnight HRV (essentially resting-state) via Apple Watch. This paper validates that resting HRV reductions are meaningful markers for depression. MAON can use these benchmarks to calibrate its z-score thresholds for flagging concerning HRV trends in users who self-report depression.
