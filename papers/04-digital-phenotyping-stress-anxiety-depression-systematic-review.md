## Summary

A systematic review of 40 studies examining how smartphone sensors can passively detect behavioral patterns associated with stress, anxiety, and mild depression. Provides evidence that digital phenotyping can identify mental health symptoms before they become severe.

## Key Findings

- Smartphone sensors effectively identify behavioral patterns associated with stress, anxiety, and mild depression
- GPS, accelerometer, screen usage, and app usage are the most commonly used passive sensors
- 78% of studies used machine learning models for prediction
- Reduced mobility, increased time at home, and altered phone usage patterns correlate with depressive symptoms
- Behavioral patterns were identified but not validated as exclusive to specific mental health conditions
