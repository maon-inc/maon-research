## Summary

Develops and evaluates machine learning methods to detect PTSD hyperarousal events using physiological and activity data from commercial smartwatches. Tested on veterans with diagnosed PTSD, demonstrating that consumer wearable data can identify hyperarousal episodes in real-world settings.

## Key Findings

- Used commercial off-the-shelf wearable devices to collect HR and accelerometry from veterans with PTSD
- ML algorithms successfully detected hyperarousal events from physiological and activity signals
- Heart rate elevations and specific movement patterns characterize hyperarousal episodes
- Consumer smartwatch data is sufficient for meaningful PTSD symptom detection
- Real-world ambulatory data collection over extended periods demonstrated feasibility
- Physiological signatures of hyperarousal are distinct from normal stress responses

## MAON Applicability

Directly relevant for MAON users who self-report PTSD. MAON's continuous HR and activity tracking via Apple Watch could detect hyperarousal episodes using similar ML approaches. MAON could flag sudden HR elevations accompanied by specific activity patterns as potential hyperarousal events.

## Methodology Notes

Published in PLOS ONE (2022). Veteran sample with diagnosed PTSD. Used commercial smartwatch (not research-grade). ML classification of hyperarousal events. Open access.
