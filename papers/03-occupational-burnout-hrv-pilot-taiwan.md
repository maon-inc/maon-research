## Summary

A pilot study examining the association between occupational burnout and HRV in high-tech company employees in Taiwan. Demonstrates autonomic nervous system imbalance in workers experiencing burnout.

## Key Findings

- Occupational burnout was significantly associated with reduced HRV parameters
- Workers with higher burnout scores showed lower parasympathetic activity
- The LF/HF ratio (sympathetic-parasympathetic balance) was altered in burned-out workers
- HRV measurements can distinguish varying levels of burnout severity
- Tech industry workers are a high-risk population for detectable autonomic changes

Relevant for MAON users in knowledge work/tech who report stress or burnout. MAON's overnight HRV tracking can identify the autonomic patterns described here as burnout indicators, especially the sustained sympathetic dominance pattern.

## Methodology Notes

Pilot study in a high-tech company in Taiwan. Published in Medicine (2020). Cross-sectional design. Relatively small sample but directly relevant to MAON's likely user demographic.
