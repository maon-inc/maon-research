## Summary

Reviews the role of physical activity in ADHD management, covering diagnostic applications (accelerometry), digital interventions, and exercise as treatment across the lifespan.

## Key Findings

- Moderate-high physical activity improves executive function in children with ADHD
- Exercise correlates with better working memory, inhibitory control, and information processing
- Accelerometer-measured activity data provides objective ADHD assessment
- Digital health platforms can deliver exercise-based ADHD interventions
- Physical activity benefits for ADHD persist across the lifespan

## MAON Applicability

Supports MAON recommending physical activity specifically for ADHD users as an evidence-based symptom management strategy. MAON can track whether ADHD users meet activity thresholds associated with cognitive benefits and nudge toward movement when activity declines.
