## Summary

A systematic review and meta-analysis of chatbot-delivered interventions for improving mental health outcomes among young people. The review identified 29 eligible interventional studies, including 13 randomized controlled trials, and conducted meta-analyses on chatbot effectiveness for reducing psychological distress, depression, and anxiety.

## Key Findings

- Meta-analysis showed that chatbot-delivered interventions significantly reduced distress in young people across the pooled studies
- 29 eligible studies were identified, with 13 being randomized controlled trials providing higher-quality evidence
- Chatbots appear effective across multiple mental health domains including depression, anxiety, and general psychological distress
- The evidence base is growing rapidly, with most studies published in 2022-2025
- Effect sizes varied considerably across studies, suggesting moderating factors such as chatbot design, therapeutic approach, and target population
- Both rule-based and AI-powered chatbots showed benefits, though comparison between types was limited

## MAON Applicability

Provides meta-analytic evidence supporting MAON's AI-driven conversational approach for mental health support. While MAON targets a broader age range, the finding that chatbot-based interventions effectively reduce distress in young people validates the general approach of delivering mental health support through automated conversational interfaces. The variation in effect sizes highlights the importance of MAON's personalization and proactive outreach design.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review following PRISMA guidelines. 29 studies included, 13 RCTs. Meta-analysis performed on RCT data. Heterogeneity was notable across studies. Publication bias assessment included. Young people defined broadly across the included studies. Search covered major databases through mid-2024.
