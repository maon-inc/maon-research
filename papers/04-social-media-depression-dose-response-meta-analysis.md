## Summary

A dose-response meta-analysis of 26 studies (55,340 participants) examining the relationship between social media use time and depression risk. Finds a linear dose-response relationship with 13% increased depression risk per additional hour of social media use.

## Key Findings

- Linear dose-response: depression risk increases 13% per hour of additional social media use
- Overall pooled odds ratio of 1.59 for high vs. low social media use
- Cross-sectional studies: OR = 1.61; longitudinal studies: OR = 1.57
- Association is particularly strong for girls
- Passive consumption of social media is more harmful than active engagement

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks screen time by app category including social media. This paper provides quantitative thresholds for when social media use becomes concerning. MAON could flag when daily social media time exceeds levels associated with elevated depression risk.

## Methodology Notes

Dose-response meta-analysis published in IJERPH (2022). 26 studies, 55,340 participants. Includes both cross-sectional and longitudinal designs. Primarily adolescent populations. Open access.
