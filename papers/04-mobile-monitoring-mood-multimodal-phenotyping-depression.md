## Summary

This observational longitudinal study (Mobile Monitoring of Mood) examined multimodal digital phenotyping in patients with major depressive episodes (including major depressive disorder, bipolar disorder, and concurrent borderline personality disorder) compared to healthy controls. The study passively collected data on smartphone screen and app use, communication patterns, sleep, mobility, and physical activity. A key finding was that group-level differences in any single modality remained relatively modest, highlighting the need for multimodal data fusion.

## Key Findings

- Among outpatients with mild to moderate depressive disorders, group-level differences from healthy controls in any single data modality (screen use, mobility, sleep, etc.) remain relatively modest.
- Combining data from multiple modalities is essential to detect more subtle differences and identify individualized behavioral signatures of depression.
- The study examined temporal patterns of smartphone screen and app use, communication, sleep, mobility, and physical activity across diagnostic groups.
- Patients with concurrent borderline personality disorder showed distinct digital phenotyping patterns compared to those with major depressive disorder alone.
- Individual-level variability was substantial, suggesting that personalized baselines rather than population norms may be more clinically useful.
- The multimodal approach provides a comprehensive behavioral profile that maps to multiple MAON data domains simultaneously.

## MAON Applicability

Directly supports MAON's multi-domain cognitive score approach, which combines health data (steps, sleep, HR, HRV), screen time, calendar density, and location visit patterns into a single composite score. The finding that no single modality suffices validates MAON's design decision to integrate multiple data streams. The study's focus on screen time, mobility, and sleep patterns maps directly onto MAON's feature set. The emphasis on personalized signatures aligns with MAON's approach of building individual baselines over time.

## Methodology Notes

Observational longitudinal study comprising 3 subcohorts of patients with major depressive episodes (MDD, bipolar disorder, and concurrent BPD) plus healthy controls. Used passive smartphone sensing for data collection across multiple behavioral modalities. Published in JMIR Mental Health, volume 12, 2025. Study conducted in a real-world outpatient setting, enhancing ecological validity.
