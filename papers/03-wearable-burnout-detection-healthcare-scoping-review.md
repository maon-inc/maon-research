## Summary

A scoping review examining how wearable technologies can detect burnout and well-being in healthcare professionals. Reviews physiological markers including HR, HRV, sleep, and skin temperature as indicators of burnout states.

## Key Findings

- Heart rate, HRV, sleep quality, and skin temperature all vary in response to occupational stress and burnout
- Short-term changes in burnout symptoms are associated with disturbances in heart rate measures
- Wearable technology may support early identification of those at risk for burnout before clinical manifestation
- Proactive wearable monitoring outperforms retrospective survey-based burnout assessment
- Up to 60% of healthcare workers experience burnout symptoms, making early detection critical

## MAON Applicability

Directly validates MAON's stress/burnout detection via HR and HRV signals. MAON could detect burnout trajectory from sustained elevated resting HR and reduced HRV trends over weeks, proactively reaching out before users self-identify burnout.

## Methodology Notes

Scoping review published in JMIR (2024). Focuses on healthcare populations but physiological markers generalize. Covers multiple wearable device types and signal modalities.
