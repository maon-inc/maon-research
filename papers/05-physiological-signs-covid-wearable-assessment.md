## Summary

Assesses physiological signs associated with COVID-19 as measured by Fitbit wearable devices. Identifies specific patterns in HR, SpO2, sleep, and activity that distinguish infected from non-infected individuals.

## Key Findings

- COVID-19 infection produces measurable changes in resting heart rate, SpO2, sleep duration, and activity
- Elevated resting heart rate relative to personal baseline is an early indicator
- Changes in sleep architecture and duration accompany infection
- SpO2 decreases may indicate respiratory involvement
- Consumer-grade Fitbit devices capture clinically meaningful physiological changes

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks all the signals examined here (HR, SpO2, sleep, activity) via Apple Watch. The specific patterns identified (RHR elevation, SpO2 changes, sleep disruption, activity decrease) give MAON concrete multi-signal anomaly patterns for illness detection.
