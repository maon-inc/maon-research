## Summary

A PNAS review examining the sleep-circadian interface as a diagnostic and therapeutic window into mental disorders. Argues that sleep and circadian disruption are transdiagnostic features of psychiatric conditions.

## Key Findings

- Sleep and circadian disruption are transdiagnostic features across psychiatric conditions
- The sleep-circadian interface provides a mechanistic link between biology and psychopathology
- Circadian rhythm disruption is both a risk factor and consequence of mental disorders
- Sleep-circadian interventions show promise as transdiagnostic treatments
- Wearable monitoring of sleep-circadian patterns could improve psychiatric care

## MAON Applicability

Positions MAON's sleep and circadian monitoring as a transdiagnostic tool applicable across all conditions users self-report. Supports monitoring sleep-circadian features for all users regardless of specific condition.
