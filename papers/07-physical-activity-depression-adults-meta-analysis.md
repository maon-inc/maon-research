## Summary

A systematic review and meta-analysis specifically examining physical exercise as intervention for major depressive disorder in adults. Provides effect sizes and modality comparisons.

## Key Findings

- Physical exercise significantly reduces symptoms of major depressive disorder
- Effect sizes are clinically meaningful across multiple exercise types
- Both aerobic and resistance training show benefits
- Exercise benefits persist across different depression severity levels
- Combined exercise programs may offer the largest effects

## MAON Applicability

Reinforces MAON's evidence base for promoting physical activity to users who self-report depression. MAON can reference this meta-analytic evidence when nudging users to maintain or increase activity levels.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review and meta-analysis published in Scientific Reports (2023). Focuses specifically on MDD (clinical depression). Adult population. Open access.
