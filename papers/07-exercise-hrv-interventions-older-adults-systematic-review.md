## Summary

A systematic review examining how different exercise types affect HRV and cardiovascular health. Finds that aerobic exercise is most effective at improving HRV, while resistance training has weaker effects on autonomic function.

## Key Findings

- Aerobic/endurance exercise most effectively improves HRV (parasympathetic function)
- Resistance training alone has weaker effects on cardiac autonomic control
- Exercise training leads to improved (increased) HRV in healthy individuals
- Post-exercise HRV recovery time correlates with training status
- High HRV relative to baseline indicates healthy autonomic adaptability

## MAON Applicability

Informs how MAON interprets the interaction between exercise patterns and HRV trends. If a user increases aerobic exercise and MAON observes concurrent HRV improvement, this validates the exercise benefit. If exercise increases but HRV does not improve, it may signal overtraining or other issues.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review published in European Review of Aging and Physical Activity (2021). Covers multiple exercise types and their HRV effects. Open access.
