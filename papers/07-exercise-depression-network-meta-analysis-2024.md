## Summary

A landmark network meta-analysis comparing exercise modalities, doses, and intensities against psychotherapy and antidepressants for depression treatment. Finds medium effect sizes for exercise with specific modality recommendations.

## Key Findings

- Physical activity had medium effects on depression (median effect size = -0.43) compared with usual care
- Walking/jogging, yoga, strength training, and mixed aerobic exercise all showed significant effects
- Moderate-intensity exercise 3-4 times per week for 30-45 minutes was most effective
- Exercise effects comparable to psychotherapy and medication for mild-moderate depression
- Dose-response relationship: more intense exercise generally produced larger effects

## MAON Applicability

Provides quantitative benchmarks for MAON's activity monitoring. MAON can track whether users meet the effective dose (150+ min/week moderate activity) and flag when exercise drops below therapeutic thresholds, particularly for users who self-report depression.

## Methodology Notes

Network meta-analysis published in BMJ (2024). Includes 218 RCTs. Compares exercise against active comparators. Highest-quality evidence for exercise-depression relationship. Open access.
