## Summary

Large-scale study analyzing over 50,000 days of wearable data to examine bidirectional associations between digital markers of circadian disruption and mental health outcomes. Demonstrates that wearable-derived circadian metrics predict mood fluctuations and mental health risk.

## Key Findings

- Analysis based on over 50,000 days of wearable data reveals bidirectional links between digital markers of circadian disruption and mood
- Circadian disruption markers (sleep timing irregularity, activity pattern disruption) predict next-day mood changes
- The relationship is bidirectional: poor mood also predicts subsequent circadian disruption
- Wearable-derived circadian metrics are practical digital biomarkers for mental health monitoring
- Real-world data collection demonstrates ecological validity of approach

## MAON Applicability

Directly supports MAON's approach of using wearable-detected circadian patterns (sleep timing, activity rhythms, screen use timing) to predict mood changes. Validates that MAON should monitor circadian regularity as a key upstream mental health signal and flag disruptions proactively.

## Methodology Notes

Published in npj Digital Medicine (2024). Very large dataset (50,000+ person-days). Real-world wearable data collection. Bidirectional analysis. High-impact journal. Open access.
