## Summary

A systematic review and meta-analysis of generative AI chatbot interventions for mental health, synthesizing evidence from 26 studies (14 RCTs, n=6,314) across 11 countries. The meta-analysis provides the first pooled effect size estimate for GenAI chatbots as therapeutic tools, finding a small but statistically significant effect on reducing negative mental health symptoms including depression and anxiety.

## Key Findings

- Meta-analysis of 14 RCTs showed a statistically significant overall effect (ES=0.30, p=0.047, N=6,314, 95% CI 0.004-0.59) for reducing negative mental health issues
- Social-oriented chatbots (providing social interactions) were more effective than task-oriented chatbots (assisting with specific tasks)
- Interventions were conducted across 11 countries, with notable representation from non-WEIRD populations
- Literature search of 11 databases returned 5,555 records, with 26 studies meeting inclusion criteria
- Substantial heterogeneity was observed across studies, with moderate risk of bias
- Research gaps include lack of studies focusing on young children and older adults
- Current findings represent a foundation for more rigorous future research rather than definitive evidence of efficacy

## MAON Applicability

Relevant to MAON's in-app AI chat feature, which provides conversational support to users about their health and wellness data. The finding that social-oriented chatbots outperform task-oriented ones suggests MAON's conversational, companion-style approach may be more effective than purely directive health coaching. The small but significant effect size provides evidence that AI chatbots can meaningfully reduce mental health symptoms, supporting MAON's inclusion of chat-based interactions alongside passive monitoring.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review following PRISMA guidelines with meta-analysis. Searched 11 databases with backward citation tracking and manual searching, completed March 2025. Included studies using generative or hybrid AI chatbots delivering interventions with quantitative mental health outcomes. Meta-analysis restricted to RCTs measuring negative mental health issues with control groups. Total of 5,469 participants across 26 studies.
