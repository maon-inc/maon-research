## Summary

Uses wrist-worn actigraphy to objectively characterize activity, sleep, and circadian rhythm patterns in PTSD. Provides insights into how PTSD manifests across multiple wearable-detectable signal domains simultaneously.

## Key Findings

- PTSD patients show distinct patterns in activity, sleep, and circadian rhythms measurable via wrist actigraphy
- Sleep fragmentation and disrupted rest-activity rhythms are characteristic of PTSD
- Circadian rhythm disruption in PTSD is objectively quantifiable via wearable sensors
- Daytime activity patterns differ between PTSD and controls
- Multi-domain characterization (activity + sleep + circadian) provides richer PTSD phenotyping than any single metric
- Wrist-worn sensors are feasible for continuous PTSD monitoring in daily life

## MAON Applicability

MAON collects all three signal domains (activity via steps, sleep stages, circadian patterns via timing). This paper validates MAON's multi-signal approach for PTSD users, showing that combined activity-sleep-circadian characterization captures PTSD-specific patterns more effectively than individual metrics.
