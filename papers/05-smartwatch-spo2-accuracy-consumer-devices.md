## Summary

Validation study comparing blood oxygen saturation (SpO2) accuracy across consumer smartwatches against a clinical-grade pulse oximeter reference. Evaluates Apple Watch, Samsung Galaxy Watch, Garmin, and Fitbit SpO2 measurements.

## Key Findings

- Apple Watch Series 7 had the most accurate SpO2 measurements (MAE = 2.2%, MDE = -0.4%, RMSE = 2.9%)
- Garmin Venu 2s had the least accurate measurements (MAE = 5.8%, MDE = 5.5%, RMSE = 6.7%)
- All devices showed reduced accuracy during motion and at lower SpO2 levels
- Consumer smartwatch SpO2 is suitable for trend monitoring but not clinical-grade diagnosis
- Device accuracy varies significantly by manufacturer and model

## MAON Applicability

MAON collects SpO2 via Apple Watch. This paper establishes Apple Watch as the most accurate consumer SpO2 device, validating MAON's data quality. However, the ~2% error margin means SpO2 is best used for trend detection rather than absolute values, informing how MAON should interpret SpO2 data.

## Methodology Notes

Published in PLOS ONE (2023). Compared 4 consumer smartwatch brands against clinical pulse oximeter reference. Tested under various conditions. Open access.
