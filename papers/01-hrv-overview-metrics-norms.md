## Summary

A comprehensive overview of HRV metrics including SDNN, RMSSD, and pNN50 with normative values and clinical interpretation guidelines. This reference paper explains which HRV measures best capture different aspects of autonomic function relevant to mental health monitoring.

## Key Findings

- SDNN is the "gold standard" for medical stratification of cardiac risk and reflects overall autonomic variability
- RMSSD is the primary time-domain measure for estimating vagally-mediated HRV changes and is preferred over pNN50 for parasympathetic assessment
- pNN50 indicates parasympathetic-to-sympathetic balance but is less reliable at low HRV values
- Age, sex, and fitness level significantly affect normative HRV ranges, requiring personalized baselines
- Short-term (5-min) and long-term (24-hr) recordings yield different normative values

## MAON Applicability

Essential reference for MAON's HRV interpretation engine. MAON should use RMSSD as its primary vagal tone indicator and SDNN for overall autonomic assessment. Personalized z-scores (which MAON already computes) are validated by this paper's emphasis on individual baseline variation.
