## Summary

Demonstrates that wearable devices can automatically detect persistent physiological changes following COVID-19 infection, with implications for long COVID management and recovery monitoring.

## Key Findings

- Persistent physiological changes detectable via wearables after COVID-19 infection
- Some individuals show prolonged elevation in resting heart rate post-infection
- Recovery trajectory varies significantly between individuals
- Automatic detection algorithms can identify individuals with incomplete physiological recovery
- Wearable monitoring enables longitudinal tracking of post-infection recovery

## MAON Applicability

Extends MAON's illness detection to recovery monitoring and long-term health tracking. MAON could identify users whose physiological baselines have not returned to pre-illness levels, flagging incomplete recovery that may warrant attention.

## Methodology Notes

Published in Scientific Reports (2025). Uses consumer wearable data for long COVID detection. Longitudinal analysis. Open access.
