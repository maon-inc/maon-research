## Summary

A comprehensive scoping review identifying 133 studies on digital health technologies (DHTs) for adults with ADHD, searching databases through December 2025. The review categorizes DHTs into three functions: treating a specific condition, driving clinical management, and diagnosing a specific condition, mapping the full landscape of digital interventions for adult ADHD.

## Key Findings

- 133 eligible studies were identified, making this one of the most comprehensive reviews of digital ADHD technologies to date.
- Treatment-focused studies (63) most commonly used web/app-based cognitive therapy or psychoeducation (n=26), cognitive training programs (n=13), transcranial stimulation (n=12), and neurofeedback (n=9).
- Clinical management studies (36) primarily supported diagnostic decision-making through machine-learning analysis of continuous performance tasks, neuroimaging, and virtual reality data.
- Two studies examined haptic feedback wearables to improve symptoms of anxiety and focus in ADHD adults.
- Web or app-based cognitive therapy/psychoeducation was the most frequently studied technology type (29 papers total).
- The review reveals growing evidence for digital self-management tools but highlights gaps in real-world wearable-based interventions.

## MAON Applicability

Highly relevant to MAON's role as a digital health companion for users who may have ADHD. MAON's combination of screen time tracking, app blocking, and AI-driven insights maps onto the "treat specific condition" and "drive clinical management" categories identified in this review. The finding that app-based psychoeducation is the most studied modality validates MAON's approach of delivering personalized insights and summaries through a mobile interface.

## Methodology Notes

Scoping review following PRISMA-ScR guidelines. Searched PubMed, Embase, Web of Science, and CENTRAL databases through December 2025. Studies were included if they evaluated a digital health technology in adults with diagnosed or suspected ADHD. 133 studies met inclusion criteria.
