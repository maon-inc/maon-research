## Summary

A review of how wearable devices and biometric data can detect and monitor viral infections. Covers the full spectrum from pre-symptomatic detection through disease progression to recovery monitoring.

## Key Findings

- Viral infections produce detectable changes in HR, HRV, temperature, respiratory rate, SpO2, sleep, and activity
- Changes can appear before symptom onset in many individuals
- Multi-signal approaches outperform single-signal detection
- Recovery monitoring through signal normalization is feasible
- Consumer wearables provide sufficient signal quality for infection detection

## MAON Applicability

Provides comprehensive framework for MAON's illness detection beyond COVID-19. The multi-signal anomaly patterns described (elevated HR + temperature + disrupted sleep + reduced activity) apply to general viral infections, making MAON's illness detection broadly applicable.
