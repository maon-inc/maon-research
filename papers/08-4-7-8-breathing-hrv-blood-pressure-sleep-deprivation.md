## Summary

A laboratory study of 43 healthy young adults (19-25 years) compared the immediate effects of the 4-7-8 breathing technique on HRV and blood pressure between sleep-deprived and non-sleep-deprived groups. Three sets of 4-7-8 breathing immediately reduced heart rate and systolic blood pressure in both groups and increased high-frequency HRV (parasympathetic marker) in the control group, demonstrating rapid autonomic calming from the technique. Published at Burapha University, Thailand.

## Key Findings

- Three sets of 4-7-8 breathing immediately decreased heart rate and systolic blood pressure in all participants
- High-frequency HRV power (parasympathetic activity) significantly increased post-exercise in the non-sleep-deprived group
- Low- and very-low-frequency HRV powers decreased in the non-sleep-deprived group, reflecting sympathetic withdrawal
- Sleep-deprived participants showed attenuated HRV improvements, suggesting sleep debt blunts parasympathetic recovery
- Endothelial function was significantly lower at baseline in sleep-deprived individuals
- The technique's autonomic benefits are most pronounced in well-rested individuals, supporting sleep as foundational

## Methodology Notes

Controlled laboratory study, N=43 (22 sleep-deprived, 21 control), ages 19-25. Sleep deprivation defined as under 5 hours the prior night. HRV measured via standard time-domain and frequency-domain metrics. Participants performed 3 sets of 4-7-8 breathing. Published July 2022, Physiological Reports (Physiological Society, open access). Burapha University, Faculty of Allied Health Sciences, Thailand.
