## Summary

A dose-response meta-analysis examining both sedentary behavior and physical activity as risk factors for depression. Provides quantitative thresholds for sedentary time and activity levels associated with depression risk.

## Key Findings

- High sedentary behavior (10.60+ hours/day) associated with 19% higher depression risk
- WHO guideline of 150-300 min/week moderate-vigorous activity reduces depression risk by 29%
- Same activity level reduces anxiety risk by 20%
- Inverse dose-response: more activity = lower depression risk, with diminishing returns
- Sedentary behavior is an independent risk factor even when controlling for exercise

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks both active minutes and can infer sedentary time. The quantitative thresholds (10.6h/day sedentary = risk, 150+ min/week MVPA = protective) give MAON concrete cutoffs for flagging concerning patterns and providing evidence-based nudges.

## Methodology Notes

Dose-response meta-analysis published in Frontiers in Psychology (2025). Objectively measured via accelerometer. Includes both sedentary and activity dose-response curves. Open access.
