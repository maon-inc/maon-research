## Summary

A comprehensive review of polyvagal theory from physiological observation to clinical application. Describes the hierarchical organization of autonomic states mediated by the vagus nerve and their relevance to emotional regulation.

## Key Findings

- Polyvagal theory proposes a hierarchy of autonomic states: social engagement, fight/flight, shutdown
- Vagal tone (indexed by HRV/RSA) reflects capacity for emotional regulation
- Higher vagal tone associated with better social engagement and stress resilience
- Lower vagal tone associated with anxiety, depression, and trauma responses
- Theory provides framework for understanding autonomic-emotional connections, though some claims remain debated

## MAON Applicability

Provides a conceptual framework for MAON to interpret HRV and respiratory patterns together. When MAON detects low vagal tone (low HRV + shallow breathing), it can frame wellness suggestions around vagal stimulation (breathing exercises, social connection).
