## Summary

This four-week longitudinal study directly examined associations between smartwatch-measured HRV metrics and self-reported depression and anxiety symptoms in healthy adults. It bridges the gap between clinical HRV research and real-world consumer wearable monitoring.

## Key Findings

- Smartwatch HRV metrics showed significant associations with self-reported depression and anxiety symptoms over four weeks
- Real-time HRV collection via consumer smartwatch provided meaningful mental health signals
- Longitudinal monitoring revealed within-person temporal associations between HRV changes and symptom fluctuations
- Consumer wearable data quality was sufficient for detecting mental health-relevant patterns

## MAON Applicability
