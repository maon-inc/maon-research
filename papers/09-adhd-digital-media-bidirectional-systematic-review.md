## Summary

A systematic review of 28 studies examining longitudinal associations between digital media use and ADHD symptoms. Finds evidence for bidirectional associations: ADHD increases media use, and media use may worsen ADHD symptoms.

## Key Findings

- Bidirectional relationship: ADHD increases digital media use, and digital media use may worsen ADHD symptoms
- Individuals with ADHD are more sensitive to immediate reward feedback, driving longer screen time
- Lower self-control in ADHD increases inclination toward more frequent, prolonged digital media engagement
- Excessive and unstructured screen time associated with worsening inattention and hyperactivity
- Sleep disruption mediates part of the screen time-ADHD symptom relationship

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks screen time by app category and can detect escalating usage patterns. For ADHD users, MAON should monitor total screen time, app-switching frequency, and late-night usage as potential symptom indicators. Screen time increases may signal ADHD symptom flares.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review published in European Child & Adolescent Psychiatry (2022). 28 longitudinal studies. Covers multiple digital media types. Recent and comprehensive.
