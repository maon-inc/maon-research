## Summary

A systematic review examining physical activity patterns in eating disorders, including compulsive exercise, hyperactivity, and activity-based energy expenditure measured by accelerometers.

## Key Findings

- Anorexia nervosa patients show significantly higher daily walking durations and step counts than controls
- Compulsive exercise is a distinct behavioral pattern measurable by accelerometer
- Activity patterns differ by eating disorder subtype
- Excessive exercise serves compensatory functions in bulimia and binge eating
- Wearable monitoring of activity patterns can identify concerning exercise behaviors

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks steps and active energy, enabling detection of compulsive exercise patterns. For eating disorder users, MAON should flag both extremes: abnormally high activity (compulsive exercise) and abnormally low activity (sedentary restriction behavior). Context-specific interpretation is critical.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review published in Nutrients (2020). Covers multiple eating disorder subtypes. Includes accelerometer-measured activity. Open access.
