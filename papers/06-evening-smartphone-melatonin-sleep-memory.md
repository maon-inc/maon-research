## Summary

An experimental study examining the effects of evening smartphone use (with and without blue-light filters) on melatonin levels, sleep architecture, and declarative memory consolidation in male adolescents and young adults. The study found that smartphone use without a blue-light filter significantly suppressed melatonin, with age-dependent recovery patterns.

## Key Findings

- Reading on a smartphone without a blue-light filter significantly suppressed melatonin levels in both adolescents and young adults
- Adolescents demonstrated faster recovery from melatonin suppression before bedtime, while adults still showed significantly reduced melatonin at bedtime
- Adults showed a slight reduction in deep sleep (N3) during the first part of the night after unfiltered smartphone use, an effect not observed in adolescents
- Blue-light filters partially mitigated melatonin suppression but did not fully eliminate it
- The study supports sleep hygiene recommendations to avoid LED screens in the last hour before bedtime
- Memory consolidation effects were subtle but present, particularly in the adult group

## MAON Applicability

Directly relevant to MAON's screen time monitoring and its relationship to sleep. The app tracks both screen time categories and sleep quality, and the proactive AI agent can identify when evening phone use may be degrading sleep. This paper provides mechanistic evidence (melatonin suppression) supporting MAON's approach of alerting users about screen time habits. The age-dependent findings could inform personalized messaging in the onboarding and proactive agent prompts.

## Methodology Notes

Experimental crossover design with controlled laboratory conditions. Participants were male adolescents (16-17 years) and young adults (21-23 years). Three conditions: smartphone without filter, smartphone with filter, and printed text control. Melatonin measured via saliva samples. Sleep monitored by polysomnography. Published in Brain Communications (Oxford). Limitation: small sample size, male-only participants.
