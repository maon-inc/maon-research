## Summary

A systematic review examining momentary physiological indices related to eating disorders, focusing on five wearable-measurable signals: heart rate, HRV, skin temperature, EDA, and acceleration. Most directly relevant paper for MAON's eating disorder monitoring.

## Key Findings

- Five key physiological indices measurable by wearables relate to eating disorder states
- Heart rate and HRV are the most studied and informative signals for eating disorders
- Machine learning classifiers using wearable data accurately classified 91% of eating disorder behaviors
- Physiological changes precede and accompany eating disorder episodes
- Wearable sensors are feasible for real-time eating disorder behavior detection

## MAON Applicability

Core reference for MAON's eating disorder monitoring capability. While MAON lacks EDA, it has HR, HRV, and activity data. The 91% ML classification accuracy demonstrates that wearable-based eating disorder detection is achievable with the signals MAON collects.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic and methodological review published in Nutrients (2024). Covers five physiological modalities. Evaluates wearable feasibility. Open access.
