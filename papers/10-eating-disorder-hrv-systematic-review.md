## Summary

A systematic review examining HRV patterns across eating disorder subtypes. Finds that binge eating and loss-of-control eating are associated with lower resting HRV, while anorexia nervosa shows altered autonomic flexibility.

## Key Findings

- Binge eating and loss-of-control eating associated with lower resting HRV
- Anorexia nervosa shows blunted sympathetic reactivity and reduced parasympathetic flexibility
- HRV measures normalize after weight restoration in anorexia
- HRV correlates with severity of eating behaviors across subtypes
- HRV may serve as a physiological marker of eating disorder symptom severity

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks HRV continuously. For users who self-report eating disorders, MAON can monitor HRV patterns as indicators of symptom severity and recovery progress. Specifically, HRV normalization can signal positive treatment response.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review published in Psychiatry International (2023). Covers multiple eating disorder subtypes. Connects HRV to specific eating behaviors. Open access.
