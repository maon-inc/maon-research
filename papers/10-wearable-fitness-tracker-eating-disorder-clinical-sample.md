## Summary

Examines the perceived influence of wearable fitness trackers on eating disorder symptoms in a clinical sample with binge eating and restrictive eating diagnoses. Provides insights for safe wearable use with ED populations.

## Key Findings

- Clinical eating disorder patients perceive fitness trackers as influencing their symptoms
- Some patients report trackers reinforcing restrictive or compulsive exercise behaviors
- Others find objective data helpful for reality-testing distorted body perceptions
- The impact varies by eating disorder subtype and individual factors
- Healthcare providers should assess tracker use as part of eating disorder treatment

## MAON Applicability

MAON must account for the dual nature of wearable data for ED users. Some users may benefit from objective monitoring, while others may be harmed by it. MAON's conversational AI approach (rather than displaying raw metrics) may mitigate risks by controlling how data is presented.

## Methodology Notes

Published in Eating and Weight Disorders (2022). Clinical sample with diagnosed eating disorders. Qualitative and quantitative assessment. Transdiagnostic sample.
