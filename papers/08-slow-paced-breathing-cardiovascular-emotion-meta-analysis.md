## Summary

A meta-analysis and systematic review examining the effects of slow-paced breathing on cardiovascular function (HR, HRV, blood pressure) and emotional outcomes (anxiety, stress, mood).

## Key Findings

- Slow-paced breathing significantly improves multiple HRV parameters
- Cardiovascular benefits include reduced heart rate and blood pressure
- Emotional benefits include reduced anxiety and improved mood
- Effects are dose-dependent: longer practice sessions produce larger effects
- Both acute (single session) and chronic (multi-week) benefits observed

## MAON Applicability

Provides evidence base for MAON to recommend breathing exercises with specific cardiovascular and emotional benefits. MAON could track both the respiratory rate changes and HRV improvements to validate that breathing interventions are working for individual users.

## Methodology Notes

Meta-analysis and systematic review published in Mindfulness (2023). Covers both cardiovascular and emotional outcomes. Multiple study designs included.
