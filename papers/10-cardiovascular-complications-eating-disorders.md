## Summary

Reviews cardiovascular complications of eating disorders including bradycardia, orthostatic hypotension, and autonomic instability. Provides assessment guidelines relevant to wearable monitoring.

## Key Findings

- Cardiovascular complications are among the most serious medical risks of eating disorders
- Bradycardia, QT prolongation, and autonomic instability are common
- Heart rate monitoring is critical for medical safety in eating disorder patients
- Resting tachycardia in anorexia nervosa can paradoxically indicate cardiovascular distress
- Regular cardiovascular assessment is recommended throughout treatment and recovery

## MAON Applicability

MAON should apply extra caution when interpreting HR data for eating disorder users. Both abnormally low HR (bradycardia from restriction) and abnormally high HR (cardiovascular distress) are meaningful signals. MAON should alert users to seek medical attention for extreme HR values.
