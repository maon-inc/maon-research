## Summary

A preliminary study using Bayesian mixed-effects regression to analyze longitudinal wearable data for burnout prevention. Examines how daily lifestyle choices and physiological factors (sleep stages, nocturnal stress) contribute to coping capacity.

## Key Findings

- Increased daily stress correlated with higher nocturnal stress and reduced deep sleep
- Active hours promoted deeper, more restorative sleep
- Alcohol negatively affected REM sleep, particularly at moderate intake
- Bayesian approach quantifies uncertainty in effects, providing probabilistic interpretation
- Mixed-effects model accounts for both population trends and individual differences

## MAON Applicability

Highly relevant to MAON's multi-signal pattern detection. ## Methodology Notes

Preliminary study published in Frontiers in Digital Health (2025). 1-week longitudinal design with wearable data. Bayesian mixed-effects model. Small sample size but methodologically rigorous. Open access.
