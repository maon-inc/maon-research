## Summary

Large dose-response meta-analysis of 11 international cohorts examining the relationship between physical activity and anxiety risk. Found an L-shaped relationship with maximum benefit at 30 MET-h/week and a 16% anxiety risk reduction.

## Key Findings

- L-shaped dose-response relationship between physical activity and anxiety risk
- Maximum beneficial dose: 30 MET-h/week, corresponding to ~16% anxiety risk reduction
- Significant benefit begins within WHO-recommended range (10-20 MET-h/week)
- For adults over 50, activity below 14.5 MET-h/week showed no significant anxiety reduction
- Body sensitivity to physical activity for anxiety reduction may decrease with age
- Physical activity shows medium effect on anxiety (median effect size = -0.42)

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks active energy and exercise minutes that can be converted to MET-hours. This paper provides specific dose-response thresholds MAON can use to assess whether a user's activity level is in the anxiety-protective range, and suggest activity increases when falling below beneficial thresholds.

## Methodology Notes

Published in eClinicalMedicine/The Lancet (2025). 11 international cohorts. Dose-response meta-analysis. Large sample size across cohorts. Open access.
