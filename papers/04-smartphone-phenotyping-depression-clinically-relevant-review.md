## Summary

A systematic review examining how smartphone digital phenotyping data translates into clinically relevant depression predictions. Evaluates the pipeline from raw sensor data through feature engineering to clinical prediction models.

## Key Findings

- Digital phenotyping depression prediction models are moderately successful
- ML models explain approximately 41% of variance in depression scores and 31% in anxiety scores
- Missing data is a major challenge in real-world smartphone phenotyping
- Lack of external validation limits generalizability of current models
- GPS/location, app usage, and communication patterns are the most informative features
- Random forest, SVM, and deep learning are the most common ML approaches

## MAON Applicability

Provides benchmarks for what MAON's behavioral signal layer can realistically predict. The 41% variance explained for depression is a useful baseline. Also highlights the importance of MAON's multi-signal approach (combining behavioral with physiological data) to improve beyond behavioral-only models.
