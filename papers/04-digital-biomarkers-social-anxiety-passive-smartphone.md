## Summary

Demonstrates that passive smartphone sensor data can predict social anxiety symptom severity through digital biomarkers. Uses phone usage patterns, location data, and communication logs as features.

## Key Findings

- Passive smartphone sensor data accurately predicts social anxiety symptom severity
- Phone usage frequency and patterns distinguish anxiety levels
- Location mobility patterns (number of places visited, time at home) correlate with social anxiety
- Communication patterns (calls, texts) serve as social engagement proxies
- Digital biomarkers can supplement or replace periodic clinical assessments

## MAON Applicability

Validates MAON's use of phone pickup frequency and app usage patterns as anxiety indicators. The finding that reduced mobility + increased phone use signals social anxiety gives MAON a specific multi-signal pattern for anxiety detection.

## Methodology Notes

Published in JMIR (2020). Uses passive smartphone sensors. Ecological momentary design. Covers social anxiety specifically. Open access. Well-cited in digital phenotyping literature.
