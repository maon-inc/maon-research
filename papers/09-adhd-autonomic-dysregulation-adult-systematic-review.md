## Summary

A systematic review of autonomic nervous system dysregulation in adult ADHD. Examines evidence for sympathetic-parasympathetic imbalance and its relationship to core ADHD symptoms.

## Key Findings

- Mounting evidence for sympathetic-parasympathetic imbalance in adult ADHD
- Autonomic dysfunction may contribute to hyperactivity, impulsivity, and emotional dysregulation
- Emotional dysregulation in adult ADHD has measurable physiological correlates
- HRV measures show task-dependent differences in ADHD vs. controls
- Parent-reported emotion regulation is associated with HRV in ADHD

## MAON Applicability

Validates that autonomic signals (HRV) have specific relevance for ADHD users. MAON should interpret HRV patterns differently for ADHD users, particularly during emotionally charged periods detected through behavioral signals.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review published in Cognitive Neuropsychiatry (2023). Focuses on adult ADHD (relevant to MAON's user base). Covers multiple autonomic measures.
