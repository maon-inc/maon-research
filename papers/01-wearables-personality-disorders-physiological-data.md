## Summary

This narrative review examines how physiological data from consumer wearables (including HRV, HR, and electrodermal activity) can be integrated into mental health treatment, specifically for personality disorders characterized by emotional dysregulation.

## Key Findings

- Consumer wearable physiological data can meaningfully augment mental health treatment
- Real-time HRV and HR monitoring provides objective indicators of emotional dysregulation episodes
- Wearable data can help identify patterns invisible to patients and clinicians in traditional settings
- Integration requires careful attention to data quality, interpretation frameworks, and clinical workflows

## MAON Applicability

Validates MAON's broader approach of using consumer wearable data for mental health pattern recognition. The paper's framework for integrating physiological signals with subjective reports maps well to MAON's combination of biosignals with user self-reports and conversational data.

## Methodology Notes

Narrative review published in Frontiers in Psychiatry (2025). Focuses on personality disorders but findings generalize to emotional dysregulation across conditions. Discusses practical implementation challenges relevant to consumer apps.
