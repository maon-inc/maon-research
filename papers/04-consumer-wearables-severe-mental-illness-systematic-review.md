## Summary

Systematic review evaluating the utility of consumer-grade wearable devices for inferring physical and mental health outcomes specifically in severe mental illness (SMI) populations, including schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, and major depression. Assesses what consumer wearables can realistically detect in these populations.

## Key Findings

- Consumer wearables can capture meaningful health signals in severe mental illness populations
- Sleep, activity, and heart rate data from consumer devices correlate with symptom severity
- Feasibility and adherence in SMI populations is generally acceptable
- Activity and sleep disruptions detectable by wearables precede mood episodes
- Limitations include accuracy concerns compared to research-grade devices
- Consumer wearables show promise for continuous remote monitoring between clinical visits

## MAON Applicability

Validates that MAON's consumer-grade Apple Watch data collection is viable even for users with severe mental illness. Confirms that the signals MAON tracks (sleep, HR, activity) are meaningful in these populations and can complement clinical monitoring.

## Methodology Notes

Published in JMIR Mental Health (2025). Systematic review focused specifically on consumer-grade (not research-grade) devices. Evaluated SMI populations. Open access.
