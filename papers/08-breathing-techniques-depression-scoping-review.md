## Summary

A scoping review of 30 studies identifying, categorizing, and synthesizing breathing interventions evaluated for depression treatment. The review proposes a classification framework for breathing techniques based on thematic synthesis, organizing them into categories related to attention/awareness, body/sensory engagement, and paced breathing to guide clinicians and researchers.

## Key Findings

- Identified 30 papers evaluating breathing interventions for depression, spanning diverse therapeutic modalities
- Thematic synthesis revealed three main categories: attention and awareness-based breathing, body and sensory engagement breathing, and paced breathing techniques
- Slow-paced breathing at approximately 6 breaths per minute was consistently associated with improvements in depressive symptoms
- Breathing exercises integrated into broader programs (e.g., yoga, mindfulness-based cognitive therapy) showed stronger effects than standalone breathing
- The proposed classification framework provides a standardized way to describe and compare breathing interventions across studies
- Many studies lacked detailed descriptions of the breathing protocols used, limiting reproducibility

## MAON Applicability

Directly relevant to MAON's breathing session feature. This classification framework can inform the design and variety of guided breathing exercises offered in the app. MAON currently offers breathing sessions, and this review supports expanding the repertoire with different breathing types (awareness-based, paced, body-focused) that may target different aspects of depressive symptoms. The classification system could help MAON label and personalize breathing recommendations.

## Methodology Notes

Scoping review following established methodology with 30 included papers. Published in Counselling and Psychotherapy Research (2024). Used thematic synthesis to analyze descriptions of breathing interventions. Focus was on categorizing intervention features rather than measuring effect sizes.
