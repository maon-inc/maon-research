## Summary

A narrative review synthesizing evidence from published meta-analyses on the effectiveness of text messaging (SMS) interventions across key health domains, including physical activity, diet and weight management, mental health, and substance use. The review highlights the unique advantages of SMS as a delivery mechanism for health behavior change interventions.

## Key Findings

- SMS interventions have demonstrated effectiveness in mental health, physical activity, diet/weight loss, and substance use based on multiple published meta-analyses
- Text messaging offers real-time capability to collect information and deliver microdoses of intervention support throughout the day
- SMS overcomes engagement and attrition barriers common in app-based interventions by leveraging an already-used communication channel
- The simplicity of SMS belies its impact: low-tech delivery combined with evidence-based content produces meaningful behavior change
- Bidirectional (interactive) messaging shows stronger effects than unidirectional (broadcast) approaches
- SMS interventions can be personalized based on real-time sensor data, ecological momentary assessments, or user responses to create just-in-time adaptive interventions

## Methodology Notes

Narrative review of published meta-analyses rather than a primary meta-analysis. Covers multiple health behavior domains with varying levels of evidence quality. The breadth of coverage provides a useful overview but depth on any single domain (e.g., mental health specifically) is limited compared to domain-specific systematic reviews.
