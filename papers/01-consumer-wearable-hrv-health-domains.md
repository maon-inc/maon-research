## Summary

This large-scale study across five longitudinal cohorts examined resting HRV measured by consumer wearables and its associations with diverse health domains including mental health. It provides population-level evidence for the utility of consumer-grade HRV monitoring.

## Key Findings

- Resting HRV measured by consumer wearables had small-to-moderate associations with clinically oriented health measures including depressive symptoms
- Associations were consistent across five independent longitudinal studies
- Consumer wearable HRV data showed trait-like stability suitable for health monitoring
- Both time-domain and frequency-domain HRV measures from wearables were informative

## MAON Applicability

Provides strong population-level validation that MAON's consumer wearable HRV measurements are meaningful health indicators. The multi-cohort design strengthens confidence in using Apple Watch HRV data for pattern recognition across diverse health domains.

## Methodology Notes

Multi-cohort study across five longitudinal datasets published in Sensors (2025). Uses consumer wearable devices. Large combined sample size. Examines multiple health domains simultaneously.
