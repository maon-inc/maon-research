## Summary

This validation study compares six commercial wrist-worn sleep trackers (including Apple Watch Series 8, Fitbit Charge 5, Fitbit Sense) against polysomnography in 62 adults. It provides the most comprehensive recent accuracy data for consumer wearable sleep staging.

## Key Findings

- All devices detected >90% of sleep epochs but had lower wake detection specificity (29-52%)
- Apple Watch Series 8 achieved Cohen's kappa of 0.53 for sleep stage classification (moderate agreement)
- Fitbit Sense (kappa=0.42) and Fitbit Charge 5 (kappa=0.41) showed fair agreement
- Apple Watch underestimated deep sleep by 43 minutes and overestimated light sleep by 45 minutes
- REM sleep detection: Apple Watch MAPE of 52.58%, Fitbit Charge 5 MAPE of 29.69%
- Devices can effectively track prolonged and significant changes in sleep architecture

## MAON Applicability

Critical calibration data for MAON's sleep quality model. MAON should account for Apple Watch's systematic biases (deep sleep underestimation, light sleep overestimation) when computing sleep architecture metrics. Trend detection is more reliable than absolute values.

## Methodology Notes

Prospective validation study with 62 adults in sleep laboratory. Published in SLEEP Advances (2025). Single-night design. Compares six commercial devices against PSG gold standard.
