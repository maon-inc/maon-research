## Summary

This study found that day-to-day variability in step count predicted the onset of depressive symptoms 7 days before they occurred in bipolar patients, while deep sleep duration changes predicted episodes 4 days ahead.

## Key Findings

- Day-to-day variability in steps anticipated depressive symptom onset 7.0 days before occurrence
- Deep sleep duration changes predicted episodes 4.0 days ahead
- Activity variability detected mood transitions earlier than sleep or mood self-reports
- Increased variability (not just level changes) is the key predictive signal

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks steps daily and deep sleep nightly. This paper suggests MAON should compute variability metrics (e.g., coefficient of variation over 7-day windows) for both activity and sleep, as increased variability signals upcoming mood episodes in bipolar users.

## Methodology Notes

Published in Journal of Affective Disorders (2025). Longitudinal wearable monitoring of bipolar disorder patients. Prospective design tracking from variability changes to symptom onset.
