## Summary

A comprehensive review of wearable technology applications for ADHD monitoring and intervention. Covers accelerometry for hyperactivity, sleep tracking, and digital health interventions.

## Key Findings

- All wearable ADHD studies rely on accelerometry to quantify motor activity
- Wearables can detect hyperactivity through continuous activity data, sleep patterns, and fidgeting
- Actigraphy detects greater motor activity and sleep disturbances in ADHD vs. controls
- ADHD youth show longer sleep onset latency, more nighttime movement, and lower sleep efficiency
- Wearable technologies enable real-time monitoring that complements traditional diagnostic methods

## MAON Applicability

Directly validates MAON's value proposition for ADHD users. MAON's Apple Watch can serve as a continuous ADHD monitoring tool via activity patterns, sleep quality, and daily rhythm regularity. This review confirms that consumer wearables capture ADHD-relevant signals.
