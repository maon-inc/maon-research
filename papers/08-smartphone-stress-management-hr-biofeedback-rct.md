## Summary

A three-arm randomized controlled trial evaluating an 18-day smartphone-based stress management intervention (MT-StressLess) with integrated heart rate biofeedback using built-in phone accelerometer sensors. Compared the biofeedback-enhanced version against a version without biofeedback and a waitlist control. The intervention combining stress management techniques with HR biofeedback was more effective in reducing depressive symptoms than the control condition.

## Key Findings

- The smartphone-based intervention with HR biofeedback significantly reduced depressive symptoms compared to waitlist control
- Heart rate biofeedback was delivered using the phone's built-in accelerometer sensors rather than requiring external wearables
- Secondary outcomes included improvements in emotion regulation skills and overall well-being
- The 18-day intervention duration was sufficient to produce measurable effects on stress-related outcomes
- Built-in smartphone sensors provided adequate biofeedback quality for the intervention
- Usability ratings were favorable, supporting real-world deployment feasibility

## MAON Applicability

Highly relevant to MAON's stress management and breathing features. Demonstrates that brief, app-based interventions combining stress management with physiological biofeedback can meaningfully reduce depressive symptoms. MAON has access to richer biofeedback data through Apple Watch and Garmin HRV, which could enable even more precise biofeedback-guided interventions. Supports the value of MAON's approach of integrating wearable data with proactive wellness coaching.

## Methodology Notes

Three-arm RCT published in Scientific Reports (2025). Compared (1) MT-StressLess with HR biofeedback, (2) MT-StressLess without biofeedback, and (3) waitlist control. 18-day intervention period. Measured stress, depressive symptoms, emotion regulation, and well-being at pre, post, and follow-up timepoints.
