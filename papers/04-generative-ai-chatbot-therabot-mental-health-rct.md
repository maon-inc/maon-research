## Summary

The first randomized controlled trial of a generative AI therapy chatbot (Therabot) in 210 adults with clinically significant depression, generalized anxiety disorder, or eating disorder risk. Therabot, fine-tuned by clinical experts on GPT-4, produced clinically meaningful symptom reductions across all three conditions after 8 weeks, with therapeutic alliance ratings comparable to human therapists.

## Key Findings

- Therabot users showed a 51% reduction in major depressive disorder symptoms vs. waitlist controls at 8 weeks
- Generalized anxiety disorder symptoms declined 31% vs. controls over the same period
- Clinically high-risk eating disorder symptoms fell 19% relative to baseline, significantly exceeding controls
- Average engagement exceeded 6 hours of use over 8 weeks, indicating strong retention
- Therapeutic alliance ratings on validated scales were comparable to those reported in human therapy
- Benefits were maintained at follow-up, suggesting durability beyond the active intervention phase

## MAON Applicability

MAON uses an LLM (Kimi K2.5) for conversational wellness coaching via iMessage. This trial provides the highest-quality evidence to date that generative AI chatbots can produce clinically significant mental health improvements. The finding that alliance ratings match human therapists supports MAON's conversational, empathetic texting style. The eating disorder finding is also relevant to MAON's nutritional data tracking domain.

## Methodology Notes

Preregistered RCT (Dartmouth, Geisel School of Medicine; senior author Nicholas Jacobson). N=210 adults with MDD, GAD, or clinically high risk for feeding and eating disorders. Published March 2025 in NEJM AI (New England Journal of Medicine group). First RCT demonstrating clinical-level symptom reduction from a fully generative AI therapy chatbot.
