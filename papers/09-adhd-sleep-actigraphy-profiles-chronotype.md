## Summary

Uses two-week actigraphy to compare sleep profiles of children with and without ADHD. Demonstrates increased sleep fragmentation and delayed sleep phase in ADHD, detectable via consumer-grade accelerometry.

## Key Findings

- 58% of ADHD subjects met criteria for delayed sleep phase disorder
- Sleep fragmentation index significantly higher in ADHD (16.7 +/- 4.5 vs 10.3 +/- 4.4)
- Longer sleep onset latency and lower sleep efficiency in ADHD
- Delayed melatonin onset accompanies the delayed sleep phase
- Actigraphy effectively detects these sleep differences over multiple nights

## MAON Applicability

MAON can detect the delayed sleep phase and increased fragmentation typical of ADHD via Apple Watch sleep tracking. For ADHD users, MAON should specifically monitor sleep onset time drift, fragmentation index, and overall sleep efficiency as condition-specific markers.

## Methodology Notes

Published in Brain Sciences (2021). Two-week actigraphy monitoring. Compares ADHD to controls. Includes chronotype assessment. Open access.
