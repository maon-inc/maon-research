## Summary

This systematic review examines the three-way relationship between interoception (body awareness), vagal tone (indexed by HRV), and emotional regulation. It provides a framework for understanding how HRV connects to emotional and psychological wellbeing.

## Key Findings

- Higher vagal tone is associated with better emotional regulation capacity and psychological flexibility
- Interoceptive awareness mediates the relationship between vagal tone and emotional outcomes
- Low vagal tone predicts difficulty with emotional regulation across multiple clinical conditions
- The vagal tone-emotional regulation link applies across depression, anxiety, PTSD, and chronic conditions

## MAON Applicability

Provides theoretical grounding for why MAON's HRV monitoring is relevant to emotional wellbeing. When MAON detects low HRV trends, it can contextualize this as reduced emotional regulation capacity, informing the tone and content of its wellness nudges.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review published in Frontiers in Psychology (2020). Covers clinical and non-clinical populations. Integrates physiological and psychological perspectives. Open access.
