## Summary

Systematic review of 48 studies using AI models with wearable biosensor data to predict mental health conditions. Covers sensor fusion approaches combining HR, HRV, EDA, accelerometry, and digital phenotyping signals for depression, anxiety, stress, and other conditions.

## Key Findings

- 48 studies identified using AI with wearable biosensors for mental health prediction
- Most effective biomarkers: heart rate and step count for depression; sleep patterns and social interaction for anxiety
- 76% of studies used single-device approaches; multi-device fusion shows higher accuracy but is underexplored
- High bias risk from small samples and limited external validation across studies
- Group-level differences between mild-moderate depression and controls are relatively modest in single modalities
- Combining multiple signal modalities is needed to detect subtler individual differences

## MAON Applicability

Directly validates MAON's multi-signal approach. MAON fuses HR, HRV, sleep, activity, and digital behavior data. This review confirms that combining these modalities improves mental health detection over any single signal, supporting MAON's architecture of cross-domain pattern recognition.
