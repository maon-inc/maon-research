## Summary

Examines the effect of slow breathing on anxiety regulation with physiological measurements. Demonstrates that slow breathing significantly reduces state anxiety through parasympathetic activation.

## Key Findings

- Slow breathing significantly more effective than regular breathing for reducing state anxiety
- Slow breathing buffers against anxiety increases following aversive stimuli
- Parasympathetic activation is the primary mechanism of anxiety reduction
- Effects are measurable through HRV and self-report measures
- Practical intervention requiring no equipment beyond breath pacing

## MAON Applicability

MAON could guide users through slow breathing exercises during detected stress episodes. MAON's respiratory rate tracking could confirm whether guided breathing successfully lowered the user's breathing rate, providing feedback on intervention adherence.

Published in Scientific Reports (2025). Experimental design. Measures both physiological and subjective outcomes. Open access.
