## Summary

Reviews the role of late-night eating in circadian disruption and its downstream effects on depression and emotional health. Connects eating timing to mood regulation via circadian mechanisms.

## Key Findings

- Late-night eating disrupts circadian rhythms through food-entrained oscillators
- Circadian disruption from late eating increases depression risk
- Metabolic consequences include insulin resistance and weight gain
- Eating timing affects cortisol and melatonin rhythms
- The circadian-eating-mood connection is bidirectional

## MAON Applicability

MAON can detect late-night activity patterns that may indicate nighttime eating. Combined with its sleep and mood tracking, MAON can identify the circadian disruption pattern (late eating + poor sleep + mood decline) and suggest meal timing adjustments.
