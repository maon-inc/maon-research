## Summary

A living umbrella review synthesizing 24 systematic reviews that together encompass 249 non-duplicate validation studies of consumer wearable devices involving 430,465 participants. The review evaluates accuracy across multiple biometric outcomes including heart rate, step count, sleep, SpO2, and arrhythmia detection, finding that only about 11% of commercially available wearables have been validated for at least one biometric outcome.

## Key Findings

- Of all commercially available wearable devices released to date, approximately 11% have been validated for at least one biometric outcome
- Heart rate measurement showed a mean bias of plus or minus 3%, which is generally acceptable for consumer health applications
- Arrhythmia detection exhibited pooled sensitivity of 100% and specificity of 95%
- Step counting accuracy varies significantly by device and activity type, with greater error during non-ambulatory activities
- Sleep staging accuracy remains lower than polysomnography, though total sleep time estimates are reasonably accurate
- SpO2 measurement accuracy from wrist-worn devices is limited, particularly at lower saturation levels
- The review will be continuously updated as new evidence emerges (living review protocol)

## MAON Applicability

Provides a comprehensive evidence base for understanding the accuracy limitations of the biometric data MAON ingests from Apple Watch and Garmin devices. The review's findings on HR accuracy (plus or minus 3%) and sleep measurement limitations should inform how MAON weights these inputs in its cognitive score calculations. The 11% validation rate highlights why MAON should build in uncertainty margins and avoid over-interpreting small day-to-day changes in wearable metrics.

## Methodology Notes

Living umbrella review registered on PROSPERO (CRD42023402703). Searched databases through 2024, identifying 24 systematic reviews meeting inclusion criteria. Total participant pool of 430,465 across 249 unique validation studies. Published in Sports Medicine (IF ~9.3). The living review protocol ensures periodic updates with new evidence.
