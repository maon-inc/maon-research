## Summary

A systematic review and meta-analysis examining the relationship between social jetlag (the discrepancy between social and biological time) and mental health outcomes. Finds a statistically significant association between social jetlag and depressive symptoms.

## Key Findings

- Statistically significant correlation between social jetlag and depressive symptoms
- Subjects with 2+ hours of social jetlag show more severe depressive symptoms
- Circadian misalignment may overactivate the HPA axis and shift cortisol rhythms
- Gender moderates the relationship (different patterns for males and females)
- Effect size is small but consistent across studies

## MAON Applicability

MAON can detect social jetlag by comparing weekday vs. weekend sleep timing patterns from Apple Watch data. When social jetlag exceeds 2 hours consistently, MAON could flag this as a risk factor and suggest sleep schedule regularization.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review and meta-analysis published in Acta Psychologica (2025). Covers multiple populations and age groups. Quantifies effect sizes. Recent publication.
