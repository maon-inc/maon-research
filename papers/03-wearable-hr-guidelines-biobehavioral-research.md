## Summary

Provides comprehensive guidelines for using wrist-worn consumer wearables to assess heart rate in biobehavioral research. Covers device selection, data collection protocols, artifact handling, and analysis best practices.

## Key Findings

- Wrist-worn PPG devices provide valid HR measurements under controlled conditions but accuracy degrades with motion
- Nocturnal/resting measurements are most reliable for biobehavioral research
- Data cleaning and artifact rejection protocols are essential for valid results
- Consumer devices differ significantly in sampling rate, algorithm transparency, and data access
- Standardized reporting guidelines are needed for wearable HR research

## MAON Applicability

Essential reference for MAON's data quality pipeline. The guidelines on when HR data is most/least reliable (nocturnal > daytime, rest > motion) should inform MAON's confidence weighting. The artifact handling recommendations apply directly to MAON's preprocessing.
