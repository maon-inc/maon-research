## Summary

A randomized controlled trial evaluating the effectiveness of a rule-based, topic-specific chatbot intervention for improving mental health self-care, literacy, and well-being. The study enrolled 285 participants across chatbot intervention (n=140) and waitlist control (n=145) groups, with the chatbot delivering 10 topic-specific sessions over 10 days covering stress management, emotion regulation, and value clarification.

## Key Findings

- Chatbot group showed significantly greater improvements in behavioral intentions and mental health literacy compared to controls
- Significant improvements observed in self-care behaviors, mindfulness, depressive symptoms, overall well-being, and positive emotions after 10 days
- Improvements did not differ significantly from control at 1-month follow-up, suggesting effects may not sustain without continued engagement
- Topic-based sequential delivery (one session per day) helped structure the intervention and maintain engagement
- The 7-day free-access period after the structured sessions showed declining usage, highlighting the challenge of sustained engagement
- Results suggest that chatbot design needs greater personalization and interactive features to enhance long-term outcomes

## Methodology Notes

Randomized controlled trial with 285 participants. Intervention: 10-day structured chatbot sessions plus 7-day free access. Measures taken at baseline, post-intervention (10 days), and 1-month follow-up. Self-report measures for mental health literacy, self-care efficacy, well-being, and depressive symptoms. Published in JMIR. Limitation: waitlist control design, self-report outcomes.
