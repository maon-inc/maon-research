## Summary

An overview of systematic reviews examining physical activity interventions for depression, anxiety, and distress. Provides the broadest evidence synthesis of exercise as a mental health intervention.

## Key Findings

- Physical activity reduces symptoms of depression, anxiety, and psychological distress across populations
- Effects are consistent across age groups, genders, and clinical severity levels
- Higher intensity exercise generally produces larger effects
- Benefits observed even for short-duration interventions
- Exercise is effective as both standalone treatment and adjunct to pharmacotherapy/psychotherapy

## MAON Applicability

Provides strong evidence base for MAON to recommend physical activity as a wellness intervention. MAON can contextualize activity level declines with evidence that exercise has broad mental health benefits across the conditions users self-report.

## Methodology Notes

Overview of systematic reviews published in British Journal of Sports Medicine (2023). Umbrella review covering the broadest evidence base. High methodological quality.
