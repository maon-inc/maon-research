## Summary

This review explores how integrating artificial intelligence with smartphone technology can enhance personalized assessment and treatment for eating disorders. It covers smartphone-based monitoring tools, digital phenotyping, natural language processing for symptom detection, and the potential for closed-loop adaptive interventions that respond to real-time behavioral and physiological signals.

## Key Findings

- Smartphone technology offers a scalable path to expanding access to evidence-based eating disorder assessment and treatment
- Digital phenotyping and natural language processing may detect eating disorders in novel ways beyond traditional self-report
- Closed-loop adaptive interventions can respond to real-time signals to deliver personalized support at moments of vulnerability
- The egosyntonic nature of eating disorder symptoms (e.g., valuing low body weight, dietary restraint) can diminish motivation to engage with digital interventions
- Skepticism toward AI technologies and fatigue from continuous self-monitoring are significant barriers to adoption
- Combining passive sensing data with active self-report creates more comprehensive behavioral profiles for intervention tailoring
