## Summary

Systematic review synthesizing methodologies, evaluation practices, and ethical/governance frameworks of LLM-based chatbots developed for mental health counseling. Searched Google Scholar, PubMed, IEEE Xplore, and ACM Digital Library for studies from January 2020 to May 2025. Specifically examines the emergence of agentic AI architectures that go beyond simple conversation to incorporate tool use, memory, and multi-step reasoning.

## Key Findings

- LLM-based mental health chatbots show promise for scalable and personalized support, but current evidence suffers from heterogeneous study designs and minimal external validation
- Agentic AI architectures that integrate wearable data, health databases, and tool-calling capabilities represent a significant advance over purely conversational systems
- Safety and governance practices are inconsistently reported across studies, with most lacking formal risk mitigation frameworks
- Few studies evaluated long-term therapeutic outcomes; most focused on single-session or short-term engagement metrics
- Prompt engineering and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) are the dominant customization approaches, with fine-tuning less common due to cost and data requirements
- The review identifies a critical gap in standardized evaluation frameworks that span system performance, usability, and clinical effectiveness

Directly relevant to MAON's AI chat feature and proactive agent system (sendblue worker). ## Methodology Notes

PRISMA-compliant systematic review. Inclusion criteria required original development or empirical evaluation of LLM-driven mental health counseling chatbots. Quality assessment used adapted CONSORT and CHEERS checklists. Limitations include rapid evolution of the field potentially outpacing the review timeline, and exclusion of non-English publications.
