## Summary

The World Sleep Society Sleep Tracker Task Force published landmark recommendations for the use of wearable consumer health trackers (CHTs) that monitor sleep. The document defines seven Fundamental Sleep Measures (FSMs) that every wearable should report in a standardized format, and provides pragmatic guidance for users, clinicians, researchers, and manufacturers on appropriate CHT usage and development.

## Key Findings

- Defines seven Fundamental Sleep Measures (FSMs) that all consumer wearables should report consistently: total sleep time, sleep onset latency, wake after sleep onset, sleep efficiency, time in bed, sleep stages, and regularity
- Distinguishes FSMs from proprietary exploratory metrics, noting the latter may have future clinical potential but currently lack validation
- Recommends best practices for interpreting CHT-derived sleep data in healthy individuals while acknowledging device limitations
- Explores CHT roles in evaluating and managing insomnia, sleep apnea, and circadian rhythm disorders
- Calls for manufacturer transparency in algorithm validation and standardized reporting
- Addresses risks of orthosomnia (anxiety from over-monitoring sleep data) and recommends clinician guidance for vulnerable populations

## MAON Applicability

Directly relevant to MAON's Apple Health sleep data integration and cognitive score system, which uses sleep duration as a core input. The FSM framework validates MAON's approach to tracking sleep duration and quality, and supports expansion into additional standardized sleep metrics. The recommendations on clinician-consumer collaboration align with MAON's goal of translating raw health data into actionable wellness insights.

## Methodology Notes

Expert consensus recommendations developed by the World Sleep Society Sleep Tracker Task Force, composed of leading sleep researchers and clinicians. Based on systematic review of existing validation literature across consumer sleep tracking devices. Published in Sleep, the official journal of the Sleep Research Society.
