## Summary

A systematic review developing implementation guidelines for breathing practices targeting stress and anxiety reduction. Provides evidence-based protocols for integrating breathing interventions into wellness applications.

## Key Findings

- Slow, nasal, diaphragmatic breathing significantly improves vagal tone, HRV, and parasympathetic activity
- Reduces cortisol, anxiety, stress, and PTSD symptoms
- Optimal breathing rates are 4.5-6.5 breaths per minute for most adults
- Multi-session interventions show cumulative benefits over single sessions
- Guidelines applicable to digital health applications and wearable-guided interventions

## MAON Applicability

Provides implementation framework for MAON to deliver breathing interventions. The protocol guidelines (rate, duration, frequency) can be translated into MAON's conversational nudges. MAON's respiratory rate tracking can measure adherence and effectiveness.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review published in IJERPH (2023). Develops practical implementation guidelines. Covers multiple breathing techniques. Open access.
