## Summary

This meta-analysis of 14 RCTs (n=794) evaluated HRV biofeedback as an intervention for depressive symptoms. It found a medium effect size (g=0.38) for reducing depressive symptoms, supporting the bidirectional relationship between HRV and mood.

## Key Findings

- HRV biofeedback produced a medium effect size (g=0.38) for reducing depressive symptoms across 14 RCTs
- The intervention also increased HRV in participants, suggesting autonomic function can be improved
- Effects were observed across both clinical and subclinical depression populations
- Biofeedback training that increases HRV has downstream benefits for mood regulation

## MAON Applicability

While MAON does not provide biofeedback directly, this paper validates the bidirectional HRV-mood relationship. MAON can recommend breathing exercises or relaxation techniques when it detects sustained low HRV, grounding that recommendation in evidence that improving HRV improves mood.

## Methodology Notes

Meta-analysis of 14 randomized controlled trials with 794 total participants. Published in Scientific Reports (2021). Includes both clinical and non-clinical populations.
