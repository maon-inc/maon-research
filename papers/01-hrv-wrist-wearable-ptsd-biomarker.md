## Summary

This study specifically used wrist-worn wearable devices to measure HRV as a biomarker for identifying adverse neuropsychiatric outcomes following traumatic stress exposure. It demonstrates that consumer-grade wrist wearables can capture meaningful HRV signatures related to PTSD risk.

## Key Findings

- Wrist-wearable HRV measurements successfully identified individuals at risk for adverse posttraumatic neuropsychiatric outcomes
- Consumer-grade devices can capture clinically meaningful HRV data for PTSD screening
- HRV patterns measured from wrist devices predicted neuropsychiatric sequelae after trauma
- Over 1 in 5 Americans already wear wrist devices capable of this measurement

## MAON Applicability

Directly validates MAON's approach of using Apple Watch HRV data for mental health pattern recognition. For users who report PTSD or trauma history, MAON can monitor HRV trends as early warning indicators of worsening neuropsychiatric symptoms.

## Methodology Notes

Published in Psychiatry Research (2024). Uses wrist-worn wearable devices (consumer-grade), making findings directly applicable to Apple Watch data collection. Prospective study design tracking outcomes after trauma exposure.
