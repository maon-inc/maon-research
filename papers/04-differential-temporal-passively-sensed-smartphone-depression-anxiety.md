## Summary

Large-scale longitudinal study (n=1,013; 74.6% female; mean age 40.9) using the LifeSense app for continuous passive smartphone data collection (GPS, app usage, device use, communication) across 16 weeks. Hierarchical linear regression tested within- and between-person associations of 2-week windows of passively sensed data with depression (PHQ-8) and generalized anxiety (GAD-7).

## Key Findings

- GPS-derived circadian movement correlated with depression at the between-person level but did not predict within-person symptom changes over time, suggesting it marks trait-level risk rather than state fluctuations
- Home duration emerged as a prospective within-person indicator of depression symptom change, meaning increased time at home preceded worsening symptoms
- Communication features (call/text vs. app-based messaging) showed differential associations with PHQ-8 and GAD-7, indicating depression and anxiety have distinct digital signatures
- Between-person and within-person effects often diverged, highlighting the importance of separating these levels in digital phenotyping research
- GPS location entropy was associated with depressive symptoms at the group level but had limited individual-level predictive utility
- The study used one of the largest samples to date (n=1,013) for passive smartphone sensing research

## MAON Applicability

Directly supports MAON's location visit tracking feature and its role in the cognitive score. The finding that home duration is a prospective within-person indicator validates MAON's approach of monitoring location routines to detect early signals of mood decline. The distinction between trait-level (circadian movement) and state-level (home duration) markers informs how MAON should weigh location signals in its scoring algorithm.

## Methodology Notes

Longitudinal observational cohort study with rolling enrollment. Participants downloaded the LifeSense app on personal Android phones. Passive data collected continuously; PHQ-8 and GAD-7 administered biweekly. Hierarchical linear models separated between-person and within-person effects. Limitations include Android-only sample and self-selected participants with elevated baseline symptoms.
