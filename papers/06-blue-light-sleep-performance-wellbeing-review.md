## Summary

A systematic review examining the effects of blue light exposure on sleep, cognitive performance, and wellbeing in young adults. Covers the mechanisms by which evening screen use disrupts circadian rhythms.

## Key Findings

- Evening blue light (460-480 nm) suppresses melatonin secretion via melanopsin-containing retinal ganglion cells
- 2-hour evening blue light exposure significantly suppresses melatonin (from 12.0 to 8.8 pg/mL)
- Blue light suppresses melatonin twice as long as green light and shifts circadian rhythms by twice as much
- Even short evening tablet/smartphone use significantly reduces melatonin and delays sleep onset
- Chronic circadian disruption linked to impaired sleep, mood disorders, and metabolic dysregulation

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks late-night screen time by app category. This paper provides the mechanistic explanation for why late-night screen use correlates with poor sleep and mood. MAON can flag late-night screen use as a circadian disruption risk factor with evidence-based guidance.

## Methodology Notes

Systematic review published in Frontiers in Physiology (2022). Covers experimental and observational studies. Focuses on young adults (MAON's likely user demographic). Open access.
