## Summary

Smart-Pomodoro is a smartwatch-based application designed to improve time management, engagement, and focus during study sessions for children with ADHD. The system uses real-time, rule-driven prompts on a smartwatch to help children plan and manage study intervals and breaks, drawing on the Pomodoro Technique. Caregivers can customize sessions with goal-oriented objectives and gamification strategies.

## Key Findings

- Children with ADHD showed improved focus and task completion when using structured smartwatch-based Pomodoro intervals compared to unstructured study sessions.
- Caregivers could configure customized study sessions, helping children mitigate ADHD symptoms and build motivation through gamification.
- Parents rated the system positively for ease of use, usefulness, and effectiveness in managing study behaviors.
- The smartwatch modality reduced distraction compared to phone-based timers, as the entry point into the task changed.
- Real-time wearable prompts supported organizational skills without being inherently distracting to the child.
- The system demonstrated feasibility for home-based ADHD behavioral intervention using consumer wearable hardware.

## Methodology Notes

Feasibility and usability study with children diagnosed with ADHD. Evaluated through caregiver surveys and behavioral observations during home study sessions. Sample included families with ADHD-diagnosed children using smartwatch hardware. Qualitative and quantitative evaluation of user experience and task outcomes.
