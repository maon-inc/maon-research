## Summary

A UK Biobank prospective cohort study using isotemporal substitution to estimate the mental health effects of replacing sedentary behavior with light or moderate-vigorous activity, measured by accelerometer.

## Key Findings

- Replacing 60 min sedentary time with light activity reduced depression scores by 1.3%
- Replacing 60 min with moderate-vigorous activity reduced depression scores by 12.5%
- Replacing 60 min with sleep reduced depression scores by 7.6%
- Even light activity replacement provides measurable mental health benefit
- Moderate-vigorous activity replacement shows the largest effect

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks active energy, exercise minutes, and can infer sedentary time. This paper provides specific substitution effects MAON can communicate: even small increases in light activity (walking) provide benefits. MAON could suggest replacing 30-60 minutes of screen time with a walk, backed by this evidence.

## Methodology Notes

Prospective cohort published in BMC Medicine (2021). UK Biobank, large sample. Accelerometer-derived activity measures. Isotemporal substitution analysis. Open access.
