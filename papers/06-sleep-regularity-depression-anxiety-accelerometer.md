## Summary

A large UK Biobank study using wrist accelerometers to measure sleep regularity, finding that regular sleepers have 38% lower depression risk and 33% lower anxiety risk over 7.5-year follow-up, independent of sleep duration.

## Key Findings

- Regular sleepers had 38% lower risk of developing depression compared to irregular sleepers
- 33% lower risk of developing anxiety in regular sleepers
- Irregular sleepers who met duration guidelines still faced elevated risks
- Sleep regularity is a stronger predictor of mental health than sleep duration alone
- Sleep regularity is modifiable and objectively trackable with wearables
- 7-day wrist accelerometry sufficient to derive meaningful sleep regularity index

## MAON Applicability

Critical finding for MAON: sleep timing regularity matters more than duration. MAON should compute a sleep regularity index from Apple Watch data and track it alongside duration. Irregular sleep timing should be flagged as a mental health risk factor independent of total sleep time.

## Methodology Notes

UK Biobank study published in Psychological Medicine (2025). Large prospective cohort (n > 50,000 implied). 7.5-year median follow-up. Accelerometer-derived sleep regularity. 1,646 incident depression cases, 2,097 anxiety cases.
