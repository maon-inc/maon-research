## Summary

A systematic review identifying the key features and sensor modalities used in digital phenotyping for monitoring mental disorders. Maps which smartphone-derived features are most predictive for which conditions.

## Key Findings

- Mobility features (GPS-derived) are most widely used and effective for mood disorders
- Higher mobility associated with mania; reduced mobility with depression
- More time spent at home correlates with more severe depressive symptoms
- Phone usage patterns (screen time, app use, pickup frequency) are key behavioral markers
- Multiple sensor fusion improves prediction over single-sensor approaches
- Standardization of measurement and reporting is urgently needed

## MAON Applicability

Maps directly to MAON's feature set: screen time by category, app pickups, and location patterns. The finding that mobility + phone usage combined outperform either alone supports MAON's multi-signal architecture. The condition-specific feature mapping helps MAON weight signals differently for different self-reported conditions.
