## Summary

Protocol for a multi-centre study using remote measurement technology (smartphones and Oura ring) to characterize eating disorder illness stages and recovery trajectories. Passively monitors daily behavior and physiology to understand predictors of recovery vs relapse.

## Key Findings

- Uses Oura ring for passive measurement of sleep, autonomic arousal, and activity in ED patients
- Multi-parametric remote measurement via smartphones and wearable smart rings
- Aims to identify digital biomarkers distinguishing earlier vs more progressed clinical presentations
- Predicting real-time onset of ED symptoms using momentary physiological indices for just-in-time interventions
- Research supports feasibility and acceptability of wearable devices in ED populations
- Could reduce relapse rates by enabling early detection of deterioration patterns

## MAON Applicability

Directly analogous to MAON's approach for ED users. Validates using consumer wearable (ring/watch) sleep and autonomic data to track eating disorder recovery trajectories. MAON could implement similar longitudinal monitoring to flag physiological patterns suggesting relapse risk.

## Methodology Notes

Published in BMC Psychiatry (2024). Multi-centre prospective cohort study protocol. Uses Oura ring and smartphone passive sensing. Longitudinal design. Open access.
