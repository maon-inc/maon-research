## Summary

Examines how smartphone notifications degrade cognitive control, demonstrating the "switch cost" of notifications on attention and task performance through both behavioral and electrophysiological measures.

## Key Findings

- Smartphone notification sounds significantly slow response times on cognitive tasks
- Notifications create "switch costs" where returning to the original task requires time and mental effort
- The effect occurs even when users do not interact with the notification
- Notification-induced attention fragmentation has measurable electrophysiological correlates
- Frequent notifications create cumulative cognitive costs throughout the day

## MAON Applicability

MAON tracks notification patterns and app pickup frequency. This paper provides the cognitive mechanism behind why high notification loads correlate with stress and reduced wellbeing. MAON could flag high notification frequency as an attention fragmentation risk factor.

## Methodology Notes

Published in PLOS ONE (2022). Experimental design with EEG and behavioral measures. Lab-based study. Demonstrates causal mechanism for notification-attention disruption. Open access.
