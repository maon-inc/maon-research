## Summary

Validates a consumer smartwatch for measuring nocturnal respiratory rate during sleep. Reports RMSE of 1.13 bpm for overnight measurements, confirming accuracy sufficient for health monitoring applications.

## Key Findings

- Consumer smartwatch nocturnal respiratory rate RMSE of 1.13 bpm for overnight measurements
- Continuous measurement RMSE of 1.62 bpm (slightly less accurate than overnight average)
- Smartwatch-derived respiratory rate is valid for sleep monitoring applications
- Nocturnal measurements are most reliable (minimal motion artifact during sleep)
- Accuracy sufficient for detecting clinically meaningful respiratory rate changes

## MAON Applicability

Directly validates MAON's use of Apple Watch nocturnal respiratory rate. The 1.13 bpm accuracy means MAON can reliably detect respiratory rate changes of 2+ bpm, which is clinically meaningful for illness detection and stress monitoring.

## Methodology Notes

Validation study published in Sensors (2023). Compares consumer smartwatch against reference respiratory rate measurement. Sleep-only measurements. Open access.
