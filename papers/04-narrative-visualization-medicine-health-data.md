## Summary

This review explores the intersection of data visualization and narrative medicine, defining four applied domains where visualization can support patient care: understanding patients from narrative records, medical communication, medical conversation training in education, and psychotherapy/emotional wellness enhancement. The paper bridges visualization research and narrative medicine to identify opportunities for using visual storytelling to present health data in more engaging, patient-centered ways.

## Key Findings

- Narrative medicine, where patient stories play a central role in diagnosis and treatment, supports a more holistic approach to care than traditional scientific methods alone
- Four applied domains are identified: narrative record understanding, medical communication, conversation training, and psychotherapy/emotional wellness
- The combination of narrative and medical data visualization represents an underexplored research opportunity
- Task-audience-based visual storytelling can improve patient comprehension of their own health data
- User-centered interactive visualization allows patients to engage more deeply with their health information
- Visual storytelling helps bridge the understanding gap between physicians and patients regarding illness experiences
