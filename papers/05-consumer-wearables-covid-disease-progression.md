## Summary

Demonstrates that consumer-grade wearables can track multi-system physiological changes throughout COVID-19 disease progression, from pre-symptomatic phase through acute illness and into recovery.

## Key Findings

- Consumer wearables track changes across multiple physiological systems during COVID-19
- Disease progression visible through HR, HRV, temperature, respiratory rate, and activity changes
- Pre-symptomatic phase shows earliest changes in resting heart rate
- Acute phase shows maximal disruption across all physiological parameters
- Recovery phase characterized by gradual normalization of signals over 1-3 weeks

## MAON Applicability

Provides a complete illness trajectory model for MAON: detection (pre-symptomatic RHR changes) -> monitoring (acute phase multi-signal disruption) -> recovery tracking (signal normalization). MAON can use this trajectory to provide appropriate messaging at each illness phase.
