## Summary

Validation study comparing heart rate and respiratory rate accuracy from two types of wearable devices against reference standards. Provides accuracy benchmarks for PPG-based respiratory rate estimation.

## Key Findings

- Wearable-derived respiratory rate measurements show moderate accuracy against reference devices
- PPG-based respiratory rate estimation is more prone to noise in wrist-worn devices
- Accuracy improves during sleep/rest compared to active states
- Respiratory rate accuracy sufficient for trend detection but not precise clinical measurement
- Combined PPG + accelerometer approaches improve respiratory rate accuracy

## MAON Applicability

Calibrates MAON's expectations for respiratory rate accuracy from Apple Watch. MAON should focus on trend changes (rising or falling respiratory rate over days) rather than absolute values, similar to its approach with other wearable-derived metrics.

## Methodology Notes

Validation study published in Sensors (2022). Compares two wearable device types. Tests accuracy across different conditions. Open access.
