## Summary

A narrative review examining the relationship between screen time and ADHD symptom severity in children and adolescents, with a focus on treatment approaches. The review synthesizes evidence that excessive and unstructured screen time is consistently associated with worsening ADHD symptoms, particularly inattention and hyperactivity, and discusses intervention strategies.

## Key Findings

- Excessive and unstructured screen time is consistently associated with worsening ADHD symptoms, especially inattention and hyperactivity.
- As screen time increased, the risk of ADHD symptom exacerbation rose significantly across multiple screen-use categories.
- The relationship between screen time and ADHD appears bidirectional: ADHD increases vulnerability to excessive screen use, while excessive screen use can worsen ADHD symptoms.
- Structured digital interventions and screen time management strategies can mitigate negative effects.
- Content type and context of screen use matter more than total duration alone in predicting symptom impact.
- Treatment approaches that combine screen time awareness with behavioral management show promise for ADHD symptom reduction.

## Methodology Notes

Narrative review of peer-reviewed literature on screen time and ADHD in youth populations. Synthesized findings from cross-sectional, longitudinal, and intervention studies. Published 2025.
