## Summary

A clinical review comparing HRV profiles in bipolar disorder and borderline personality disorder, two conditions featuring emotional dysregulation. It clarifies how autonomic dysfunction manifests differently across these disorders.

## Key Findings

- Both bipolar disorder and borderline personality disorder show reduced HRV compared to healthy controls
- HRV reductions reflect shared autonomic dysregulation underlying emotional instability
- State-dependent HRV changes differ between conditions, with bipolar showing mood-state-specific patterns
- HRV may serve as a transdiagnostic marker of emotional dysregulation severity

## MAON Applicability

Helps MAON understand that reduced HRV in the context of emotional dysregulation is a shared feature across multiple conditions. MAON should consider emotional dysregulation as a common pathway when interpreting HRV patterns, regardless of specific diagnosis.

## Methodology Notes

Clinical review published in Heart (BMJ, 2018). Compares two clinical populations. Narrative review synthesizing existing evidence.
