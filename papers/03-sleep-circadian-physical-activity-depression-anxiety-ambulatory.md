## Summary

A 2-week ambulatory assessment study examining sleep, circadian rhythm, and physical activity patterns across depressive and anxiety disorders. Demonstrates that actigraphy provides more valid assessment of these patterns than self-report questionnaires.

## Key Findings

- Depressive and anxiety disorders show distinct circadian rhythm disruptions in heart rate and activity patterns
- Actigraphy-measured circadian patterns are more strongly associated with depression/anxiety than self-reported measures
- Lower daytime physical activity and disrupted rest-activity rhythms characterize both depression and anxiety
- The diurnal amplitude of cardiovascular and activity measures is blunted in mood disorders
- Two weeks of continuous monitoring captures meaningful individual-level patterns
