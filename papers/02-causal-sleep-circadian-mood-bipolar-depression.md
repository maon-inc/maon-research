## Summary

Using longitudinal wearable device data, this study establishes causal dynamics between sleep, circadian rhythm, and mood symptoms in patients with major depression and bipolar disorder, published in The Lancet's eBioMedicine.

## Key Findings

- Causal relationships exist between sleep/circadian parameters and mood symptoms (not just correlations)
- Circadian rhythm disruption causally precedes mood symptom changes in mood disorder patients
- Wearable device data captures these causal dynamics with sufficient temporal resolution
- Sleep-circadian-mood relationships differ between unipolar depression and bipolar disorder

## MAON Applicability

Provides evidence that sleep and circadian changes CAUSE mood changes (not just correlate). This strengthens MAON's rationale for proactive outreach when it detects sleep/circadian disruptions, as these are upstream causes rather than mere symptoms.

## Methodology Notes

Longitudinal study using wearable devices, published in eBioMedicine/The Lancet (2024). Causal inference methods applied to wearable time-series data. Patients with major depression and bipolar disorder.
