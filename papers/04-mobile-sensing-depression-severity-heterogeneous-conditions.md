## Summary

A study evaluating mobile sensing-based depression severity assessment across participants with heterogeneous mental health conditions, including healthy controls (n=11), individuals with major depressive disorder (n=13), and individuals with schizoaffective disorders (n=8). The study collected communication logs, location data, and continuous audio recordings over one week per participant.

## Key Findings

- Mobile sensing features from smartphones (communication logs, location data) and a wearable audioband could assess depression severity across diagnostically heterogeneous groups
- Prior studies were limited to homogeneous populations; this study demonstrates potential generalizability across different mental health conditions
- Location-derived features and communication patterns showed associations with depression severity even in a mixed clinical sample
- The approach could complement subjective questionnaire-based assessment currently used in clinical practice
- Continuous passive data collection over one week was sufficient to capture meaningful behavioral differences related to depression
- The multi-modal approach (location + communication + audio) provided richer signal than any single data stream alone

## MAON Applicability

Relevant to MAON's multi-modal passive sensing approach, which combines location tracking, screen time monitoring, Apple Health/Garmin biometrics, and calendar data. The finding that mobile sensing can assess depression severity across heterogeneous populations supports MAON's general-purpose wellness monitoring approach rather than targeting a single diagnostic group. The one-week data collection window aligns with MAON's weekly summary generation and trend analysis cadence.

## Methodology Notes

Observational study with 32 participants across three diagnostic groups. Data collected over one week per participant using personal smartphones (communication logs, GPS) and a wearable audioband (continuous audio). Small sample size limits generalizability. The heterogeneous sample is a strength for ecological validity but reduces statistical power for within-group analyses.
