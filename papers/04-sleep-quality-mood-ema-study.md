## Summary

An EMA study with 208 adults reporting mood and sleep patterns daily for 6 consecutive weeks via mobile phones. Finds that sleep quality has a significantly larger effect on next-day mood than mood has on subsequent sleep quality.

## Key Findings

- Sleep quality has a significantly larger effect on next-day mood than the reverse direction
- Worse self-reported sleep quality was associated with greater sadness the following day
- Higher sleep quality associated with higher positive affect, mindfulness, and self-control the next day
- The sleep-to-mood pathway is stronger than the mood-to-sleep pathway
- Six weeks of daily data captures robust individual-level patterns

## MAON Applicability

Validates MAON's approach of using sleep quality as a leading indicator for next-day mood. When MAON detects a poor sleep night via Apple Watch, it can proactively anticipate mood impacts and offer preemptive support via messaging.

## Methodology Notes

EMA study published in JMIR Mental Health (2019). 208 adults, 6-week daily monitoring. Self-report via mobile phone. Bidirectional analysis. Open access.
