## Summary

Examines "circadian entropy" (irregularity in circadian rhythms) as a hidden factor linking disrupted biological rhythms to mental health disorders. Proposes circadian entropy as a measurable biomarker.

## Key Findings

- Circadian entropy (irregularity in daily rhythms) is associated with multiple mental health disorders
- Higher circadian entropy predicts worse mental health outcomes
- Circadian entropy is measurable from wearable activity and sleep data
- The concept extends beyond simple sleep timing to encompass broader rhythmic disruption
- Provides a quantifiable metric for overall circadian health

## MAON Applicability

MAON could compute a "circadian entropy" score from Apple Watch sleep timing, activity patterns, and heart rate rhythms. This single composite metric could serve as a powerful circadian health indicator alongside MAON's other scores.

## Methodology Notes

Published in Translational Psychiatry (2022). Proposes novel circadian entropy framework. Open access. Builds on large-scale wearable data analysis.
