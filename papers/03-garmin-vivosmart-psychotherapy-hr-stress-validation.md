## Summary

This study validates the Garmin Vivosmart 4 wearable for measuring heart rate and stress indices during psychotherapy sessions treating test anxiety. By comparing wearable readings to gold-standard stationary devices (ECG and EDA) across 12,330 thirty-second segments from 159 treatment sessions with 26 participants, the research evaluates whether consumer wearables can reliably capture physiological arousal during therapeutic interventions.

## Key Findings

- Analyzed 12,330 thirty-second segments from 159 psychotherapy sessions with 26 participants
- Compared Garmin Vivosmart 4 heart rate and stress rate indices against gold-standard ECG and EDA measurements
- Sessions included both emotion-focused and cognitive-oriented interventions for test anxiety
- The stress rate metric incorporates both heart rate and heart rate variability while accounting for physical activity level
- Demonstrated feasibility of using consumer wearables to track physiological response during structured therapeutic settings
- Findings support the use of wrist-worn devices for monitoring autonomic arousal changes across different psychotherapy modalities

## MAON Applicability

Validates the type of Garmin physiological data that MAON integrates through its Garmin feature module. Supports the notion that consumer wearable heart rate and stress metrics can meaningfully track emotional and autonomic states relevant to mental health interventions. Relevant to MAON's cognitive score health domain, which uses resting heart rate and HRV from Apple Health, by demonstrating that similar consumer-grade signals are informative in clinical mental health contexts.

## Methodology Notes

Prospective validation study with n=26 participants across 159 psychotherapy sessions. Used Intraclass Correlations (ICC) to compare wearable and gold-standard measurements. Sessions were conducted in a clinical setting with standardized therapeutic protocols for test anxiety.
