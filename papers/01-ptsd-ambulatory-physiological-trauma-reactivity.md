## Summary

Combines passive physiological sensing with ecological momentary assessment (EMA) to measure real-world trauma reactivity in PTSD. Found that ambulatory heart rate is a promising marker of responding to trauma reminders, capturing symptom expression as it occurs naturally.

## Key Findings

- 80 participants (39 with PTSD diagnosis) completed 3 days of ambulatory physiological assessment time-synced to EMA
- Ambulatory heart rate serves as an inexpensive, accessible marker of trauma reactivity
- Real-world measurement captures actual trauma reminders rather than lab-simulated triggers
- Contextual factors significantly affect PTSD symptom expression in daily life
- Passive physiological sensing improves upon lab-based assessment of PTSD symptoms
- Heart rate reactivity to real-world trauma cues is measurable via consumer devices

## MAON Applicability

MAON's continuous HR monitoring can detect trauma reactivity patterns in real-world settings for PTSD users. Combined with time-of-day and contextual data (calendar, location), MAON could identify situational triggers associated with physiological arousal spikes.

## Methodology Notes

Published in Psychophysiology (2025). Mixed-trauma sample, ambulatory design over 3 days. Combined passive physiological sensors with active EMA reporting. Clinically diagnosed PTSD subsample.
