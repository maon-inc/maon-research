## Summary

Examines the relationship between smartphone addiction, ADHD symptoms, and sleep quality in university students. Finds that smartphone addiction rates are high (approximately 50%) and associated with both ADHD symptoms and poorer sleep.

## Key Findings

- Smartphone addiction rates were 50.4% in males and 47.2% in females
- Smartphone addiction was associated with poorer sleep quality
- 94.8% of participants primarily used smartphones, with 49.9% for social media browsing
- ADHD symptoms correlated with higher smartphone use and sleep disturbance
- The smartphone-ADHD-sleep relationship forms a reinforcing cycle

## MAON Applicability

MAON's combined tracking of screen time, app categories, and sleep quality makes it well-positioned to detect this reinforcing cycle in ADHD users. When MAON detects simultaneous smartphone overuse + declining sleep quality, it can flag this pattern for ADHD-specific intervention.

## Methodology Notes

Cross-sectional study published in Psychology Research and Behavior Management (2024). University students. Self-report measures. Large sample. Open access.
