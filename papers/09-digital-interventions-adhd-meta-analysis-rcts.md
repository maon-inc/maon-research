## Summary

A meta-analytic review synthesizing 23 randomized controlled trials with 99 effect sizes across 1,472 participants to evaluate the efficacy of digital mental health interventions for ADHD. Found small but significant effects of digital interventions in reducing overall ADHD symptoms and inattentive symptoms specifically, with intervention type significantly moderating outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Digital mental health interventions showed small, significant effects in reducing overall ADHD symptoms (g = -0.32, 95% CI [-0.53, -0.11], p = .003)
- Significant reduction in inattentive symptoms specifically (g = -0.25, 95% CI [-0.47, -0.04], p = .020)
- Intervention type significantly moderated effects: combination-based interventions were more effective
- Neurofeedback interventions showed lower effectiveness compared to combination approaches
- Telehealth and mHealth interventions showed no significant differences from other modalities
- 23 RCTs with 1,472 participants provided a robust evidence base

## MAON Applicability

Supports MAON's approach as a digital health platform that could benefit users with ADHD. The finding that combination-based interventions outperform single-modality approaches aligns well with MAON's multi-signal strategy (HRV, screen time, activity, sleep). MAON's smart schedule feature for blocking distracting apps is particularly relevant for users with ADHD-related inattention. The proactive agent could adapt its coaching for ADHD-specific challenges.

## Methodology Notes

Meta-analytic review of 23 RCTs published in Computers in Human Behavior Reports (2025). Included 99 effect sizes across 1,472 participants. Used random-effects models. Conducted moderation analyses by intervention type, outcome measure, and informant. Assessed publication bias and study quality.
