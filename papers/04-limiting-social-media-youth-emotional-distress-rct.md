## Summary

An RCT with 220 youth (ages 17-25) comparing a 3-week social media limit of 1 hour/day against unrestricted use. Finds significant reductions in depression, anxiety, and fear of missing out in the intervention group.

## Key Findings

- Limiting social media to 1 hour/day for 3 weeks significantly reduced depression (mean decrease 2.36 points)
- Anxiety symptoms reduced by 2.35 points in the intervention group
- Fear of missing out (FOMO) also significantly decreased
- Effects observed in youth already experiencing emotional distress
- Automated daily reminders helped maintain the reduced usage

## MAON Applicability

Provides specific thresholds MAON can use: >1 hour/day of social media in emotionally distressed users is a meaningful intervention target. MAON's screen time tracking by app category enables precisely this type of monitoring and nudging.

## Methodology Notes

RCT published in Journal of Technology in Behavioral Science (2024). 220 participants aged 17-25. 3-week intervention period. Uses automated reminders for adherence.
